## DFVA REPORT: Master of Psychology (Clinical Psychology)/Doctor of Philosophy (080CL)
**Institution:** University of Melbourne | **Level:** Graduate Research (combined Masters+PhD), AQF level 10 | **Duration:** 5 years full-time, or equivalent part-time
**Assessment date:** 2026-07-02 | **Source:** https://handbook.unimelb.edu.au/2026/courses/080cl

### 1. PROGRAM PROFILE
The Master of Psychology (Clinical Psychology)/Doctor of Philosophy is a combined professional-plus-research doctoral pathway: an APAC-accredited clinical psychology training sequence embedded inside a PhD. Graduates complete the coursework, supervised clinical placement, and competency requirements needed for generalist psychologist registration and clinical psychology area-of-practice endorsement with the Psychology Board of Australia, while simultaneously producing an independent, examined thesis that makes an original contribution to clinical psychology knowledge.

Structurally this means three concurrent strands over five years: (1) postgraduate coursework in psychopathology, assessment, and evidence-based intervention; (2) extensive supervised clinical placements in which trainees assess and treat adults and children presenting with a full range of clinical severity, under registered-psychologist supervision, against professional competency standards; and (3) a substantial original research thesis, independently designed, conducted, and defended.

Typical graduates become registered psychologists with clinical endorsement, working in hospital mental health services, community and private clinical practice, university clinics, forensic and child/family services, and — via the PhD — academic, clinical-research, or clinical-leadership roles. The professional registration and dual accreditation (APAC teaching-program accreditation plus PsyBA registration) constitute a hard regulatory gate that is not substitutable by AI or by an unaccredited alternative pathway.

**Overall Section Exposure:** LOW

**Durability Assessment:** The clinical core of this program — live diagnostic judgment, therapeutic relationship, crisis risk accountability, and statutory registration — is structurally resistant to AI substitution, and the combined PhD adds an independent research-generation capability on top. The only meaningfully exposed layer is administrative/documentation overhead, which AI compresses without threatening the graduate's core value proposition; if anything it should free clinical capacity, strengthening the case for augmentation rather than displacement.

### 4. THREE THRESHOLD QUESTIONS
- **Q1:** NO — Live clinical assessment, therapeutic delivery, and legally accountable risk sign-off cannot be produced by a well-prompted AI agent; documentation support is the only meaningfully automatable slice of the graduate's output.
- **Q2:** YES — The program explicitly trains graduates to formulate cases, own clinical and risk decisions under registration accountability, and generate original research insight via the PhD thesis.
- **Q3:** YES — Demand for accountable, registered human clinical judgment is likely to rise as AI absorbs routine administrative and screening tasks, freeing capacity for higher-acuity human-delivered care while the regulatory registration moat remains intact.

## 5. ANALOGUE GRADUATE PROFILE
The most exposed 2021-2022 analogue is not the clinical psychologist but the adjacent **generalist mental-health administrative/intake coordinator or basic psychometric test-scoring assistant** — roles centred on scheduling, standardised test scoring, and report templating rather than registered clinical judgment. Tools now pressuring that narrow band include ChatGPT/Claude for clinical note drafting and summarisation, Nuance DAX and similar ambient clinical documentation tools, Q-global/Pearson automated scoring platforms, Upheal and Mentalyc for AI-assisted therapy-session notes, and generic scheduling/CRM automation in allied-health practice management software. None of these tools threaten the registered clinician's diagnostic, therapeutic, or research role; they compress the administrative periphery around it.

### 6. VERDICT
This is one of the most AI-resilient program structures in the university's portfolio: a statutorily gated clinical registration pathway combined with an independently examined research doctorate. The regulatory moat (APAC accreditation, PsyBA registration and endorsement), the irreducibly relational nature of clinical practice, and the original-research requirement of the PhD strand together place this program firmly in the RESILIENT band. The main improvement lever is not structural redesign but explicit curriculum modernisation — embedding AI-literacy and AI-governance content (dimension D5) so graduates can critically supervise the AI-assisted documentation and screening tools already entering clinical practice, and strengthening published graduate destination data (D10).

## 8. THE REDESIGNED GRADUATE PROFILE
The 2027-ready graduate of this program is a registered clinical psychologist and researcher who treats AI-assisted tools as instrumented junior staff: using ambient documentation and screening AI to reclaim clinical hours, while retaining full accountability for diagnosis, treatment, and risk decisions. They can critically evaluate an AI-generated case summary or risk flag against their own formulation, understand the governance and bias limitations of algorithmic tools in mental healthcare, and bring that critical literacy into supervision of provisional psychologists.

Their PhD strand produces genuinely novel empirical or theoretical contributions to clinical psychology, increasingly using AI-accelerated literature synthesis and data analysis as an efficiency layer without ceding methodological ownership or defended interpretation. The combination of statutory registration, deep relational clinical skill, and independent research capability keeps this graduate profile durably ahead of automation pressure — the redesign task is refinement and explicit AI-governance literacy, not structural overhaul.
