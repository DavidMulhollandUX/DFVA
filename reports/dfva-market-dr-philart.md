## 2. RECENT JOB AD SIGNALS
> **Confidence: LOW** — this is structural market knowledge synthesised from known Australian humanities-PhD labour market patterns and general sector trends, not a live job-board scrape for this assessment. Treat as directional, not a citation-grade data source.

**Signal 1 — Academic market contraction.** Continuing academic positions in humanities disciplines at Australian universities have been declining relative to PhD completion numbers for over a decade, with sessional/casual teaching absorbing a growing share of early-career academic labour rather than continuing appointments.

**Signal 2 — Policy sector demand for research-trained generalists.** Australian Public Service and state government policy units continue to recruit PhD-qualified staff (often via graduate programs) explicitly for research synthesis and evidence-based policy capability, valuing the doctorate as a generalist research-training credential rather than a narrow specialism.

**Signal 3 — Editorial/publishing sector bifurcation.** Junior editorial and copyediting roles show declining ad volume consistent with AI copyediting tool adoption; senior commissioning and editorial-judgment roles remain steady, reflecting a hollowing-out of the entry tier rather than the profession overall.

**Signal 4 — Cultural sector stability with digital skill uplift.** Museum, gallery, and archive sector job ads increasingly list digital collections management and community engagement skills alongside traditional curatorial/archival training, suggesting the sector is adapting expectations rather than shrinking headcount.

**Signal 5 — Corporate "PhD as signal" hiring.** Strategy consulting, insights, and corporate communications employers increasingly list a research doctorate (any discipline) as a differentiator for analyst and strategy-associate roles, valuing demonstrated capacity for sustained independent analytical work over specific thesis topic.

**Signal 6 — Think tank and NGO analyst competition.** Entry-level research analyst roles at think tanks and NGOs report high application volumes per opening, with employers noting the need to screen out AI-generated writing samples in application processes — an early signal of the verification-skill premium.

**Signal 7 — University research administration growth.** Research development, grants management, and research-impact roles within Australian universities have grown as institutions professionalise research funding strategy, offering a stable non-teaching academic-adjacent pathway for PhD graduates.
