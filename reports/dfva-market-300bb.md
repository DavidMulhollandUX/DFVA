## 2. RECENT JOB AD SIGNALS
> **Confidence: LOW** — This section reflects structural market knowledge of Australian/UK/US education-leadership hiring patterns as of 2025-2026, not a live job-ad scrape. Treat as directional, not quantitative.

1. **Signal 1 — Director of Learning and Teaching (Australian university sector).** Postings increasingly list "experience overseeing AI-in-curriculum policy" or "generative AI governance in learning and teaching" as desirable, reflecting universities' scramble to set institutional AI policy for both students and staff.

2. **Signal 2 — Senior Policy Adviser, state/territory education departments.** Listings continue to require doctoral or near-doctoral research credibility for senior policy tiers, but increasingly also list "data literacy" and "program evaluation design" as core selection criteria, not just "policy writing."

3. **Signal 3 — Principal/Deputy Principal (independent and systemic school sectors).** EdD or equivalent doctoral qualification is cited as advantageous but rarely mandatory; postings emphasise change leadership, wellbeing strategy, and increasingly "responsible AI use in classrooms" as a leadership competency.

4. **Signal 4 — Research-Practice Partnership Lead (university-school system partnerships).** A growing category in the UoM ecosystem and internationally (e.g., US research-practice partnership movement), explicitly seeking practitioners with doctoral research training who remain embedded in system practice — a strong structural match to the EdD's design intent.

5. **Signal 5 — Higher-ed "AI in Education" strategy roles.** New role category emerging 2024-2026 (Director/Lead of AI in Learning and Teaching) at multiple Group of Eight and UK Russell Group universities — currently filled largely by generalist ed-tech/L&T staff, representing white space an AI-literate EdD graduate could credibly claim.

6. **Signal 6 — Curriculum/assessment authority senior roles (e.g., VCAA-equivalent bodies).** Continue to seek doctoral-level research capability for senior curriculum design and assessment-validity roles, with growing emphasis on AI's impact on assessment integrity.

7. **Signal 7 — International school system leadership (Asia-Pacific, Middle East).** Strong ongoing demand for doctoral-credentialed senior leaders from Australian universities, valued for research-informed leadership credibility in system-building contexts.
