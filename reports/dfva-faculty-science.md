## 1. FACULTY PROFILE

The Faculty of Science is the portfolio's largest and most internally consistent cohort — deep disciplinary expertise (Domain Depth is maximal across the faculty) and strong decision-making, but a uniform floor on the two dimensions AI most pressures: **AI Literacy** and **Outcome Evidence**. Thirteen of its Master of Science specialisations score the identical low band on graduate-outcome evidence — not a disciplinary artefact but a faculty governance norm in which employment outcomes are treated as downstream of disciplinary mastery rather than designed in. The Job Insights data shows that norm is now the fixable part.

## 4. DESTINATION CONVERGENCE & EXPOSURE READ

Science is the **research-retention pole** of the University's destination structure. Its graduates' top employers are research institutes and teaching hospitals — CSIRO, WEHI, Peter MacCallum, the Florey, the Doherty — and the modal entry role across the faculty is **Research Assistant / Laboratory Technician**. This is the academic and research-staff on-ramp: the **least automatable** destination (irreducible research work), but also the most precarious and PhD-dependent. Automation exposure is genuinely low; the strategic risk is career-pathway fragility, not displacement.

## 6. WHAT THIS MEANS

1. Publish the existing Job Insights destination data on Science programme pages — it is the role-title outcome evidence D10 measures, and it already exists. This alone lifts Master of Data Science, Master of Environmental Science and Master of Food Science toward the RESILIENT threshold.
2. Build one shared cross-Science AI-literacy module (the faculty's uniform weak point) rather than 13 separate redesigns — the single highest-leverage move for the largest faculty.
3. Track research-vs-industry destination split, since 'Research Assistant' straddles the exposure line (launchpad to Research Scientist, or holding pattern).
