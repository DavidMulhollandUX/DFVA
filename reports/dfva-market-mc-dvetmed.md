## 2. RECENT JOB AD SIGNALS
> **Confidence: LOW** — this section reflects structural market knowledge of the Australian veterinary labour market (workforce shortage reporting, AVA/JobsAustralia commentary, corporate consolidation trends) rather than a live job-ad scrape; DFVA_MOCK is active for this environment and no real-time job board query was performed.

**Signal 1 — Persistent New-Graduate Veterinarian Shortage.** Australian veterinary practices, particularly rural and mixed practices, have reported sustained difficulty filling new-graduate and experienced-vet positions since at least 2021, with the Australian Veterinary Association repeatedly flagging workforce shortage as an industry-wide crisis rather than a cyclical blip.

**Signal 2 — Rural and Regional Incentive Schemes.** State and federal governments have introduced financial incentive programs (bonded rural placements, HECS/HELP relief schemes for rural vets) specifically to redirect graduate supply toward production-animal and mixed rural practice, reflecting a structural, not cyclical, undersupply.

**Signal 3 — Corporate Consolidation Reshaping Entry-Level Roles.** Large corporate veterinary groups (Greencross/VetPartners-style consolidators) have continued acquiring independent practices across Australia, increasing standardisation of workflows (including AI-assisted documentation and imaging tools) at the point of graduate entry.

**Signal 4 — AI Tooling Entering Practice Management Software.** Veterinary practice management platforms increasingly bundle AI features (ambient scribing, automated recall reminders, imaging pre-reads) as standard rather than premium add-ons, meaning new graduates now encounter these tools by default rather than by choice.

**Signal 5 — Mental Health and Retention as a Labour-Market Constraint.** Veterinary workforce commentary consistently identifies burnout and early-career attrition (not insufficient graduate output) as the binding constraint on practice capacity — a dynamic AI documentation/efficiency tools are explicitly being marketed to address.

**Signal 6 — Growth in Companion Animal Ownership Post-2020.** Elevated pet ownership rates since the pandemic period have sustained higher demand for companion-animal veterinary services across Australia, reinforcing durable demand for GP veterinary graduates.
