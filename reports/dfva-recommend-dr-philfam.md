## 7. ASSESSMENT REDESIGN EXAMPLES
1. **AI-Positioning Statement (all disciplines, confirmation milestone addition):** Alongside the existing confirmation document, candidates write a short (1,500-word) AI-positioning statement identifying where AI tools intersect with their specific creative/clinical practice, what they will and will not use AI for, and how their practice is positioned relative to AI-driven shifts in their professional market — assessed by the supervisory panel as part of confirmation, not as a standalone unit.
2. **Discipline-Specific AI Case Study (Production/Design/Film and Television only):** Candidates in the technical/craft disciplines complete a practical case study applying an AI-assisted production tool (e.g., an AI editing, virtual-production, or generative-VFX tool) to a component of their creative work, with a critical reflection on where the tool accelerated craft execution and where human judgment had to override or redirect it.
3. **Quantitative Methods Mini-Project (Music Psychology/Music Therapy/Creative Arts Therapies only):** A short empirical mini-study (survey, experimental, or clinical-outcomes design) completed early in candidature, assessed for methodological rigour, to build genuine quantitative research capability ahead of the main thesis/creative work.

## 8. AI GOVERNANCE AND QUALITY CONTROLS
- Require explicit disclosure of any AI-assisted tool use in creative work submitted for examination (composition, design, editing, visual art), consistent with emerging academic-integrity norms for creative doctorates, without banning legitimate AI-assisted craft workflows.
- Establish discipline-appropriate guidance distinguishing AI as a creative accelerant (acceptable, disclosed) from AI as a substitute for original authorship (not acceptable for examinable doctoral work) — calibrated separately for technical/craft disciplines versus purely original-authorship disciplines like Composition or Musicology.
- For the clinical disciplines (Music Therapy, Creative Arts Therapies), ensure any AI-governance guidance aligns with the relevant professional registration body's own AI-in-clinical-practice standards, rather than creating a parallel or conflicting institutional policy.
- Build AI-tool literacy into supervisor training as well as candidate training, since supervisors across 15 disciplines currently have no consistent institutional guidance to draw on when advising candidates about AI use in their practice.

## 10. RISKS, TRADE-OFFS, AND DEPENDENCIES
- **Risk: Diluting practice-led research autonomy.** Adding structured AI-literacy and methods requirements to a historically candidate-directed, supervisor-led doctoral model risks undermining the individualised research culture that is itself a strength (D2/D4/D7). *Mitigation:* Keep new elements light-touch (a confirmation-stage statement, a seminar stream, an optional methods unit) rather than new mandatory coursework units, preserving the practice-led core.
- **Risk: Academic labour-market constraint is not curriculum-fixable.** A structurally tight sessional/continuing academic job market for conservatoire teaching roles limits graduate outcomes regardless of curriculum improvements, and no DFVA intervention changes institutional hiring capacity across the Australian higher-education sector. *Mitigation:* Be explicit in outcome reporting and advising that non-academic creative, clinical, and cultural-leadership pathways are equally valid and increasingly the majority destination, rather than implicitly measuring success against academic employment alone.
- **Risk: Uneven resourcing across 15 disciplines.** Disciplines with smaller cohorts (e.g., Interactive Composition, Indigenous Arts and Culture) may struggle to justify dedicated resourcing for new interventions relative to larger disciplines (e.g., Music Performance, Visual Art). *Mitigation:* Design all interventions as shared, cross-disciplinary infrastructure (one AI Literacy module, one advisory panel, one outcomes-tracking system) rather than per-discipline builds, so small-cohort disciplines benefit without dedicated resourcing.

## 11. REDESIGNED GRADUATE PROFILE (2027 READY)
The 2027-ready DR-PHILFAM graduate retains everything that already makes this degree durable — deep, examined, original creative or clinical practice built over four years of sustained doctoral research — and adds a layer of explicit AI fluency and market self-awareness the current program does not provide. They know, discipline by discipline, where AI accelerates their craft and where it cannot touch the embodied, clinical, or originating core of their work, and they can articulate that positioning to examiners, employers, funders, and the professional markets — academic, clinical, creative-industry, or cultural-leadership — they enter after graduation, backed by outcome data the institution can finally show them.
