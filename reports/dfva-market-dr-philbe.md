## 2. RECENT JOB AD SIGNALS
> **Confidence: LOW** — This section reflects structural labour-market knowledge of PhD-qualified hiring patterns in business/economics fields as of the assessment date, not a live scrape of current job boards (handbook scraper is blocked by anti-bot per repo constraints; DFVA_MOCK mode). Treat as directional, not evidentiary.

**Signal 1 — Central bank PhD economist hiring.** Central banks globally (RBA, Federal Reserve regional banks, ECB, BIS) continue to run structured PhD economist graduate programs, consistently emphasising causal inference and policy-relevant modelling over generic data science skills — a durable, non-automatable hiring channel for Economics and Decision/Risk/Financial Sciences graduates.

**Signal 2 — Quant hedge fund and asset manager PhD recruiting.** Systematic asset managers (quant funds, bank quant desks) maintain dedicated PhD-only recruiting pipelines for Finance, Decision/Risk/Financial Sciences and Actuarial Studies graduates, with postings increasingly specifying "AI/ML-augmented research" experience as a plus rather than a replacement for PhD-level theoretical grounding.

**Signal 3 — Academic hiring contraction.** Multiple business school disciplines (particularly Management and Marketing) report tightening tenure-track hiring ratios relative to PhD graduate output, a trend independent of AI and driven by university budget constraints — this is the single largest employability risk factor for the academic-track cohort.

**Signal 4 — Actuarial and risk consulting steady demand.** Big-4 and specialist actuarial consultancies continue steady PhD-level hiring into model governance and capital modelling roles, driven partly by regulatory requirements (APRA, prudential standards) that mandate qualified human sign-off — a structural floor under this sub-discipline's demand.

**Signal 5 — Rise of "AI research scientist, economics/behavioural" hybrid roles.** Large tech and fintech firms increasingly post hybrid roles combining PhD-level causal inference training with applied AI/ML system design — an emerging, moderately fast-growing channel for Economics, Decision/Risk/Financial Sciences and Business Administration and Analytics graduates.

**Signal 6 — Consulting firms creating "PhD fellow" tracks.** Major strategy consultancies have introduced differentiated PhD-entry tracks distinct from standard MBA/graduate hiring, valuing the credential specifically for original-research capability applied to client problems.

**Signal 7 — Corporate behavioural science teams growing.** Consumer-facing firms are expanding in-house behavioural science/consumer insight functions staffed by Marketing and Management PhDs, partly as a counterweight to unvalidated AI-generated market research.
