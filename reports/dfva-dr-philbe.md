## DFVA REPORT: Doctor of Philosophy - Business and Economics (DR-PHILBE)
**Institution:** University of Melbourne | **Level:** Graduate Research, AQF level 10 | **Duration:** 4 years full-time, or equivalent part-time
**Assessment date:** 2026-07-02 | **Source:** https://handbook.unimelb.edu.au/2026/courses/dr-philbe

### 1. PROGRAM PROFILE
The Doctor of Philosophy – Business and Economics is a research-only doctorate in which candidates undertake an original, supervised research project culminating in a thesis and oral presentation (a confirmation/defence process typical of Melbourne PhDs). The degree is offered across eight named disciplines: Accounting, Actuarial Studies, Business Administration and Analytics, Decision, Risk, and Financial Sciences, Economics, Finance, Management, and Marketing — spanning highly quantitative fields (Actuarial Studies, Decision/Risk/Financial Sciences, Finance, Economics) through to more qualitative/behavioural fields (Management, Marketing) and applied professional fields (Accounting, Business Administration and Analytics).

There is no defined coursework curriculum; candidates instead progress through a research training pathway — typically a literature review and research proposal, a confirmation milestone, one or more publishable research papers or thesis chapters, and a final thesis examined by external assessors. Structure is set by individual supervisory arrangements and discipline norms rather than a common unit list, which is the standard shape of an AQF10 PhD.

Typical graduate destinations bifurcate sharply by discipline: academia (postdoctoral and tenure-track positions) is the traditional destination across all eight streams; central banks and government economic agencies absorb Economics and Decision/Risk/Financial Sciences graduates; quantitative asset management, actuarial and risk consultancies absorb Actuarial Studies, Finance and Decision/Risk/Financial Sciences graduates; and strategy/management consulting plus applied analytics roles absorb Business Administration and Analytics, Management, Marketing and Accounting graduates. The degree functions as a credentialing and research-capability signal that opens all four pathways, with the specific discipline strongly determining which pathway is most likely.

**Overall Section Exposure:** MEDIUM

**Durability Assessment:** The PhD's exposure profile is bimodal: the mechanical middle of the research process (literature synthesis, data wrangling, first-pass code) is under real and immediate AI pressure, while the two bookends that define a PhD — original theoretical/empirical contribution and the accountable judgment to defend it under examination — remain durable. The degree's future viability therefore hinges almost entirely on whether supervision and assessment continue to reward original contribution and defended judgment, or whether candidates (and some supervisors) allow AI to hollow out the middle in a way that leaves the final contribution thin. This is a training-quality risk more than a structural one, and it varies materially by discipline: highly quantitative streams (Actuarial Studies, Decision/Risk/Financial Sciences) face the most automatable middle but also the clearest existing norms around defended technical rigour; qualitative/behavioural streams (Management, Marketing) face less mechanical automation pressure but weaker built-in defences against AI-assisted homogenisation of literature review and thin theorising.

### 4. THREE THRESHOLD QUESTIONS
- **Q1:** NO — A well-prompted AI agent can accelerate literature review, coding, and drafting, but cannot originate and defend a novel theoretical or empirical contribution against independent expert examination, which is the defining output of the first two years of doctoral research training.
- **Q2:** YES — The entire research training pathway (proposal, confirmation, thesis, defence) is explicitly designed to train candidates to design original research systems, own methodological decisions, and generate original insight; this is the degree's core design intent, not an incidental feature.
- **Q3:** UNCERTAIN — Graduates pursuing the academic track face a genuinely contracting global job market independent of AI (structural oversupply of PhDs relative to tenure-track positions), while graduates pivoting to quant finance, central banking, or applied consulting roles are likely to become more employable as AI increases demand for people who can design, validate and be accountable for AI-augmented analysis — the net effect depends heavily on which of the two pathways a given graduate takes.

## 5. ANALOGUE GRADUATE PROFILE
The most exposed analogue graduate of ~2021-2022 is the "research assistant / early-career analyst" who spent the bulk of their time on literature summarisation, manual data cleaning, and running standard/templated statistical models without owning the research question or its defence — a profile increasingly automated by tools including **ChatGPT/GPT-4-class LLMs** for literature synthesis and first-draft writing, **Elicit** and **Consensus** for systematic literature review automation, **GitHub Copilot** and **Cursor** for statistical/econometric code generation, and **Julius AI** / **Code Interpreter**-style tools for automated data analysis and chart generation. A PhD candidate who lets these tools substitute for genuine research design and defended judgment — rather than using them to accelerate the mechanical middle while deepening the original-contribution core — converges on this exposed analogue rather than the durable doctoral profile the degree is designed to produce.

### 6. VERDICT
The Doctor of Philosophy – Business and Economics is structurally well-positioned against AI disruption — its defining mechanisms (defended original contribution, independent examination, deep discipline-specific specialisation across eight distinct fields) are close to AI-proof by design — but it currently scores MODERATE RISK rather than RESILIENT because of thin, generic-handbook-driven weaknesses in AI governance training (D5), graduate outcome transparency (D10), and curriculum currency evidence (D9); closing these three specific gaps, rather than restructuring the research training model itself, is the fastest path to RESILIENT.

## 8. THE REDESIGNED GRADUATE PROFILE
The 2027-ready Doctor of Philosophy – Business and Economics graduate uses AI tools fluently and transparently to accelerate literature synthesis, data preparation, and routine model execution — while spending the time this frees up deepening the one irreducible skill the degree exists to build: original, defensible contribution to a specialist body of knowledge, examined and stress-tested by independent experts. This graduate can articulate precisely where their judgment begins and AI assistance ends, understands the governance and failure modes of the AI tools they used in their own research, and carries transparent, published evidence of where their specific discipline's graduates land — academia, central banks, quant finance, or applied consulting — so prospective candidates and institutions alike can assess the degree's real-world payoff.
