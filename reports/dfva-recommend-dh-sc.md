## 1. DIAGNOSTIC SUMMARY
The Doctor of Science scored **17/36 — HIGH RISK** on the standard DFVA scorecard, but this number should not be read the way it would be for a coursework degree. Three dimensions (D1, D9, D10) score at or near zero purely because their underlying object — an entry-level graduate cohort, a curriculum with review cycles, and post-graduation outcome data — does not exist for an examination-only Higher Doctorate. The dimensions that *do* apply to what the DH-SC actually is (D2, D3, D4, D6, D7, and the bonus dimension B) all score at or near the ceiling, because the examination standard itself ("substantial, original contribution... authoritative standing") is the maximal expression of systems thinking, technical depth, decision-making under uncertainty, domain specialisation, and research rigour. The genuine, actionable gaps are D5 (AI literacy/governance in the examination process) and D8 (not applicable in the normal sense, but worth a light institutional note).

## 2. SCORE-TO-ACTION MAPPING

Because D1, D9, and D10 are structurally inapplicable rather than genuine weaknesses, this plan does not target them with curriculum-style interventions (there is no curriculum to intervene on). Instead, the mapping below distinguishes **true gaps to close** from **structural non-gaps to document and protect**.

| Dimension | DFVA Score | Gap Diagnosis | Recommended Intervention |
|---|---|---|---|
| D5 — AI Literacy and Governance | 1/3 | True gap: the examination process has no explicit framework for assessing AI-assisted vs. AI-independent contribution in a candidate's submitted body of work | Draft an AI-authorship provenance framework for DH-SC examination panels; require candidates to disclose AI-tool use in the underlying research/publication record |
| D8 — Human and Relational Capability | 1/3 | Not a genuine gap — this dimension measures a criterion (interpersonal/clinical/ethical practice) that is not part of what the DH-SC examines or should examine | No intervention recommended; document in the assessment methodology that this dimension is inapplicable by design for research-recognition credentials |
| D1 — Automation Exposure of Roles | 0/3 | Structural non-gap: no graduate cohort exists to expose to automation | Reaffirm and clearly communicate (in handbook and marketing) that the DH-SC is not a training pathway, preventing future misapplication of this dimension |

| D10 — Graduate Outcome Evidence | 0/3 | Structural non-gap: no graduate cohort exists to track outcomes for | Substitute a lightweight post-award impact log for DH-SC recipients (continued leadership, invited addresses, citations to examined work) |

## 7. ASSESSMENT REDESIGN EXAMPLES
Because there is no coursework to redesign, the equivalent "assessment redesign" is a redesign of the **examination submission and panel process**:

1. **AI-provenance declaration:** Candidates submitting a body of work for DH-SC examination must include a structured declaration of where and how AI tools were used in producing the underlying research, data analysis, or manuscripts — reviewed by the panel as context for judging originality.
2. **Calibration exercise for examiners:** Before each examination cycle, panel members complete a short calibration exercise reviewing sample AI-assisted vs. AI-independent research outputs, to build shared judgment on where the "substantial, original contribution" bar sits as publishing practices evolve.
3. **Post-award impact statement:** Each successful DH-SC recipient is invited (not required) to contribute a brief statement of ongoing field leadership or impact, logged for institutional evidence purposes without creating a burdensome new obligation.

---

## 8. AI GOVERNANCE AND QUALITY CONTROLS
* Require explicit AI-tool-use disclosure as part of the DH-SC submission, reviewed by the examining panel alongside the substantive scientific content.
* Maintain examiner access to research-integrity and citation-provenance tooling (e.g., citation-manipulation detection, AI-text-assistance flags) as a decision aid, never a decision-maker — final judgment remains with the expert human panel.
* Periodically (at least every 2-3 years) review and update the provenance framework as AI-assisted scientific publishing practices continue to evolve, to avoid the framework becoming stale relative to actual practice.
* Preserve strict separation between this recognition credential and any coursework/training product, so AI-driven changes to entry-level science education do not get conflated with or inappropriately applied to the DH-SC's very different function.

---

## 9. MEASUREMENT PLAN
* **Leading indicators (12 months):** AI-authorship provenance framework adopted and in active use; disclosure field completion rate for new submissions; number of examiners trained on the calibration exercise.
* **Lagging indicators (12-24 months):** Post-award impact log populated for current and backfilled recipients; peer-institution benchmarking report completed and reviewed; any qualitative feedback from examiners or recipients on the credential's perceived rigour and distinctiveness.

---

## 10. RISKS, TRADE-OFFS, AND DEPENDENCIES
* **Risk:** Adding an AI-provenance disclosure requirement could be seen as burdensome or intrusive by senior candidates with decades-long publication records. *Mitigation:* Keep the disclosure lightweight and framed as protecting the credential's value, not policing the candidate.
* **Risk:** Over-formalising the examination process risks undermining the flexible, expert-judgment-driven character that gives the DH-SC its credibility. *Mitigation:* Frame all interventions (provenance framework, calibration exercise) as decision aids for the panel, explicitly preserving final expert judgment as the deciding factor.
* **Risk:** Benchmarking against peer institutions may reveal the DH-SC examination process is under-documented relative to Oxford/Cambridge equivalents, creating reputational exposure if surfaced publicly before remediation. *Mitigation:* Conduct the benchmarking internally first and remediate gaps before any public comparison is published.

---

## 11. REDESIGNED GRADUATE PROFILE (2027 READY)
There is no graduate to redesign — the 2027-ready version of this credential is a **DH-SC examination process** whose panels can confidently and consistently distinguish AI-assisted productivity from genuine field-defining scientific originality, supported by explicit disclosure requirements and research-integrity tooling, while preserving the human expert judgment that gives "authoritative standing in the field of study" its enduring meaning.
