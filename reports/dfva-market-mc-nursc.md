|---|---|---|
| Registered Nurse (Acute) | **LOW** — physical patient care irreducible | Persistent workforce shortage in Australia; NMBA registration required |
| Registered Nurse (Chronic/Community) | **LOW** — clinical judgment + patient relationship irreducible | Aging population driving demand; chronic disease management growing |
| Mental Health Nurse | **LOW** — therapeutic relationship irreducible | Mental health nursing shortage; growing demand post-COVID |
| Critical Care Nurse | **LOW** — high-acuity clinical judgment irreducible | ICU and emergency nursing — structured career pathway |
| Clinical Nurse Specialist | **LOW** — advanced practice irreducible | Post-registration specialisation; growing with healthcare complexity |
| Nurse Educator / Researcher | **LOW** — education and research design irreducible | Academic and hospital-based education roles |

## Key Signals
- Nursing is in persistent workforce shortage in Australia — structural demand driver
- NMBA registration is a regulatory barrier to AI substitution at the point of care
- Year-long clinical placements (50pt total) ensure demonstrated clinical competence
- Digital health and AI in nursing are augmenting tools (clinical decision support, documentation) but not replacing clinical judgment
- Aging population and chronic disease prevalence are structural demand drivers
- Career progression to Nurse Practitioner, Clinical Nurse Specialist, and management roles is well-established

**Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH.** Nursing is among the most AI-resistant professions in the economy.
