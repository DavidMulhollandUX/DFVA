## 2. RECENT JOB AD SIGNALS
> **Confidence: LOW** — This section reflects structural labour-market knowledge of the Australian and global biomedical research sector as of the assessment date, not a live job-board scrape. Treat as directional, not statistically representative.

**Signal 1 — Postdoctoral Research Fellow (Oncology)**
Australian cancer research institutes (Peter Mac-type postings) continue to list postdoc roles emphasising "multi-omics data integration" and "experience with AI/ML-based analysis pipelines" as desirable, alongside traditional wet-lab technique requirements — a shift from purely bench-skill listings five years ago.

**Signal 2 — Biostatistician / Data Scientist (Population Health)**
Population health and epidemiology postings increasingly specify "proficiency with AI-assisted analytics tools" and "causal inference" as differentiators, reflecting employer expectation that routine statistical coding is now AI-augmented rather than manually scripted from scratch.

**Signal 3 — Clinical Trial Coordinator / Clinician-Researcher**
Clinical research roles in Australian teaching hospitals and MDHS-affiliated units continue to emphasise GCP (Good Clinical Practice) certification, patient recruitment experience, and regulatory compliance — largely unchanged AI-exposure profile, reflecting the physical/relational nature of this work.

**Signal 4 — Biotech R&D Scientist (Pharma)**
Global pharma R&D postings (Australian subsidiaries of multinational biotech/pharma) increasingly reference "AI-assisted drug discovery" and "computational biology collaboration" as part of the role, signalling that R&D scientists are expected to direct and interpret AI-generated candidate compounds rather than solely generate them manually.

**Signal 5 — Medical Writer / Scientific Communications**
Medical writing and regulatory affairs postings show rising emphasis on "AI-tool-assisted drafting with human QA oversight," consistent with this being one of the most AI-substitutable sub-roles in the broader biomedical career ecosystem.

**Signal 6 — Research Institute Staff Scientist**
WEHI/Doherty-type institute postings remain technique- and platform-specific (e.g. specific imaging, flow cytometry, or sequencing platform expertise), showing limited direct AI-substitution pressure but rising expectation of bioinformatic literacy as a baseline skill.

**Signal 7 — Academic Fellowship / Grant-funded Research Fellow**
NHMRC/ARC-style fellowship criteria continue to weight track record (publications, grant success, translational impact) heavily — a track record that itself depends on original data generation AI cannot produce, reinforcing the durability of the core PhD training.
