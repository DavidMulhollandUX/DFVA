## 2. RECENT JOB AD SIGNALS
> **Confidence: LOW** — this section reflects structural labour-market knowledge of the Australian arts, screen, and allied-health sectors as of the current knowledge horizon, not a live job-board scrape. DFVA_MOCK conditions apply per repo convention; no real-time Seek/Adzuna/LinkedIn query was run for this report.

**Signal 1 — Registered Music Therapist / Creative Arts Therapist roles (allied health, disability, aged care sectors)**
Steady and growing demand driven by NDIS expansion and aged-care reform in Australia, requiring AHPRA-adjacent or discipline-specific registration. Roles consistently specify "registered" or "board-eligible" status, effectively AI-proofing entry via regulatory gatekeeping.

**Signal 2 — Screen industry post-production and VFX roles (Film and Television, Design, Production disciplines)**
Australian screen production (supported by federal and state offset schemes) continues to generate technical-craft roles, but studios increasingly list "AI-assisted workflow" or "familiarity with generative tools" as a preferred skill — signalling employer expectation that technical graduates be AI-tool-fluent, not AI-naive.

**Signal 3 — Conservatoire and university sessional/casual teaching roles**
Persistent reliance on sessional and casual academic labour across Australian conservatoires and arts faculties; doctoral credentialing is close to a baseline requirement for continuing academic roles, but the continuing-role market itself is structurally constrained regardless of AI.

**Signal 4 — Independent/commissioned creative practice (composition, choreography, visual art)**
Commission and grant-funded creative work (Australia Council/Creative Australia, state arts bodies) remains the dominant income structure for practising doctoral graduates in these disciplines; this market is funding-cycle-dependent rather than AI-substitution-dependent in the near term, though generic/commercial-adjacent creative income (stock composition, generic design) is contracting.

**Signal 5 — Arts and cultural sector management roles**
Ongoing demand for curatorial, programming, and cultural-leadership roles across galleries, festivals, and performing-arts companies, with increasing emphasis on First Nations-led and community-engaged programming — a growth area for the Indigenous Arts and Culture and Social Practice and Community Engagement disciplines specifically.

**Signal 6 — Music Psychology / applied research roles**
Smaller but present market in health, wellbeing, and human-factors research applications of music psychology, adjacent to but distinct from clinical music therapy roles.
