## 7. ASSESSMENT REDESIGN EXAMPLES
1. **Confirmation-of-Candidature Trade-off Defence:** Replace a purely descriptive research proposal with a structured defence requiring candidates to justify their chosen methodology against at least two viable alternatives, addressing validity, ethical, and resourcing trade-offs under panel questioning — directly assessing D2/D4.
2. **AI-Assisted Literature Review with Critical Audit:** Require candidates to produce a literature synthesis using AI tools (e.g., Elicit, Consensus) alongside a mandatory critical audit identifying AI-introduced errors, omissions, or biased framing — directly assessing D5.
3. **Applied Policy/Practice Translation Brief:** For candidates in policy or practitioner-researcher tracks, require a capstone deliverable translating thesis findings into a real-world policy brief or school-practice implementation plan, presented to an external stakeholder panel — directly assessing D1/D8.

---

## 8. AI GOVERNANCE AND QUALITY CONTROLS
- Require disclosure and documentation of all AI tool use in thesis literature reviews, data analysis, and drafting, consistent with University research integrity policy.
- Establish a supervisor training component on evaluating AI-assisted candidate work, ensuring examiners can distinguish original contribution from AI-generated synthesis.
- Mandate critical-evaluation training on AI tool limitations (hallucination risk in citations, bias in synthesis) as part of the AI literacy module.
- Set explicit boundaries on acceptable AI use in qualitative data coding to preserve methodological validity and participant confidentiality.

## 9. MEASUREMENT PLAN
* **Leading indicators (12 months):** AI literacy module completion rate among new candidates; publication of the curriculum review cadence; number of practitioner-researcher pathway enrolments tracked; scoping progress on the quantitative methods stream.
* **Lagging indicators (12-24 months):** Published graduate destination outcomes (academic vs policy vs EdTech vs leadership placement rates); quantitative methods stream completion numbers; number of active government/NGO placement partnerships; DFVA rescore movement toward the 28/36 target.

---

## 10. RISKS, TRADE-OFFS, AND DEPENDENCIES
- **Risk: Data collection for graduate outcomes may lag behind reporting timelines given long candidature length (4 years).** *Mitigation:* Start with alumni outcome surveys covering the last 5-10 years of graduates while new tracking infrastructure is built.
- **Risk: Faculty resistance to mandating AI literacy content in a research-integrity-sensitive discipline.** *Mitigation:* Frame the module around research integrity and critical evaluation rather than AI promotion, and involve MGSE research-integrity leads in co-design.
- **Risk: Quantitative methods stream requires cross-faculty resourcing (Data Science partnership) that may be slow to formalise.** *Mitigation:* Begin with elective cross-listed units from existing Data Science graduate offerings before building bespoke content.

The 2027-ready Doctor of Philosophy (Education) graduate emerges with a documented, verifiable profile: original empirical research grounded in real educational systems, explicit AI literacy and governance competency, a visible specialisation (whether learning analytics, policy evaluation, or practice-based leadership), and a clearly marketed practitioner-researcher or policy-researcher dual-skill identity — backed by published destination data proving the degree leads to durable, AI-resistant career outcomes across academia, government, and system leadership.
