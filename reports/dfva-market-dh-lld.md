## 2. RECENT JOB AD SIGNALS
> **Confidence: LOW** — Not a live scrape (DFVA_MOCK environment; no job-ad data source connected). No job ad ever specifies "must hold LLD" as a hiring criterion — the credential is honorific, not a job-market qualification — so job-ad signals are structurally inapplicable to this program. The signals below are drawn from structural knowledge of the legal-scholarship and legal-AI-tooling landscape, not from ad-market data.

**Signal 1 — Elite legal research and drafting tools maturing rapidly.** Platforms such as Harvey, Lexis+ AI, and Casetext CoCounsel (now Thomson Reuters) are being adopted inside top law firms and law schools for drafting, doctrinal synthesis, and case-law review — compressing the time cost of producing "competent" legal scholarship output.

**Signal 2 — Law school hiring committees increasingly probe AI-assisted output.** Anecdotal and reported signals (legal academia conference discussion, law school hiring norms) show growing scrutiny of whether scholarly writing samples reflect genuine original contribution versus AI-assisted drafting — directly relevant to what an LLD examination panel must now discern.

**Signal 3 — Judicial and senior legal appointments still weight "authoritative standing" heavily.** Appointments to senior judicial and academic legal roles continue to rely on reputation, citation record, and peer recognition — the same currency the LLD certifies — suggesting the underlying scarcity signal LLD certifies has not (yet) been disrupted at the top of the field.

**Signal 4 — Legal publishing volume rising, distinguishability falling.** Growth in law review submission volume (partly AI-assisted) is reported across common-law jurisdictions, making it structurally harder for any individual body of scholarship to stand out — raising the bar for what counts as "substantial and original."

**Signal 5 — Global higher doctorate conferral rates remain very low and largely unpublicized.** Peer institutions (Oxford DCL, Cambridge LLD, Sydney LLD) confer these degrees rarely (often single digits per year, some years zero), consistent with the credential functioning as a legacy prestige marker rather than an active market signal.

## 5. CURRICULUM IMPLICATIONS

| # | Implication | Dimension | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| CI-1 | The LLD has no curriculum to update, but its examination criteria function as a de facto "curriculum" for what counts as scholarly excellence — this needs the same currency discipline other programs apply to units | D9 | Establish periodic review of examination criteria against the AI-reshaped legal scholarship landscape |
| CI-2 | AI-assisted drafting is now common in legal scholarship submissions generally; examiners lack explicit guidance on distinguishing AI-assisted competent synthesis from genuinely original contribution | D5 | Add AI-disclosure requirements and examiner guidance to the examination process |
