## 7. ASSESSMENT REDESIGN EXAMPLES
1. **AI-Augmented Literature Review with Critical Appraisal Log:** Confirmation-milestone literature review must include a documented log of which AI tools were used (e.g. Elicit, Consensus), what outputs they produced, and a written critical appraisal of AI-identified gaps/errors versus the candidate's own analysis — assessed on appraisal quality, not just review completeness.
2. **AI-Output QA Case Study (Regulatory/Medical Writing track):** For candidates on clinical/translational or regulatory-adjacent pathways, a structured assessment task requiring candidates to QA an AI-generated regulatory summary or manuscript draft against source data, identifying errors, unsupported claims, or governance risks — directly building the D5 skill the market signals demand.
3. **Bioinformatic Pipeline Supervision Exercise:** Rather than assessing candidates purely on writing analysis code from scratch, include a task where candidates must validate, debug, and defend the statistical/methodological choices in an AI-copilot-generated analysis pipeline against their own dataset — shifting assessment weight toward supervision/validation judgment (D3, D5).

## 8. AI GOVERNANCE AND QUALITY CONTROLS
- Require explicit AI-tool-use disclosure statements in thesis chapters and publications arising from the candidature, consistent with emerging journal and funder policy (NHMRC, journals adopting AI-disclosure norms).
- Establish clear guidance on acceptable vs unacceptable AI use in human-subject research contexts (e.g. AI must not be used to draft informed-consent materials without human ethics-panel review), given the clinical/population-health weight of several contributing schools.
- Mandate human-in-the-loop verification for any AI-assisted data analysis or interpretation feeding into thesis conclusions, with supervisors accountable for sign-off.
- Build AI governance awareness into the annual progress-review checkpoint, so oversight is continuous across the 4-year candidature rather than a one-off induction topic.

* **Lagging indicators (12-24 months):** Published destination-data coverage (% of graduating cohort tracked); shift in graduate placement mix toward AI-resilient roles (clinical/translational, specialist R&D) versus AI-exposed roles (generic medical writing); DFVA re-assessment score movement toward/past 28/36 RESILIENT.

## 10. RISKS, TRADE-OFFS, AND DEPENDENCIES
- **Risk:** A faculty-wide compulsory module across eleven autonomous schools/institutes may face governance friction and inconsistent buy-in given differing disciplinary cultures (basic science vs clinical vs population health).
  *Mitigation:* Pilot with 2-3 willing schools first, use early wins to build cross-institute momentum, and keep core content generic with discipline-specific add-on modules.
- **Risk:** Alumni destination tracking depends on voluntary self-report from graduates who have dispersed globally across academia, biotech, and clinical practice, risking low response rates and biased data.
  *Mitigation:* Combine survey outreach with LinkedIn/publication-trail desk research to supplement self-report and improve coverage.
- **Risk:** Over-indexing on AI-tool training could inadvertently signal to candidates and external examiners that the PhD is diluting its research-rigour core.
  *Mitigation:* Frame all AI literacy content explicitly as strengthening (not replacing) the existing rigorous primary-research training — "AI-fluent specialist," not generalist.
