## DFVA REPORT: Doctor of Physiotherapy (MC-DPHYSIO)
**Institution:** University of Melbourne | **Level:** Graduate Coursework (Masters Extended), AQF Level 9 | **Duration:** 36 months full-time
**Assessment date:** 2026-07-02 | **Source:** https://handbook.unimelb.edu.au/2026/courses/mc-dphysio

### 1. PROGRAM PROFILE

The Doctor of Physiotherapy is a three-year, full-time, 300-credit-point entry-to-practice graduate degree that produces AHPRA-registrable physiotherapists. The curriculum is organised around three professional identities — Self, Practitioner, and Advocate — supported by nine elements: Professional Practitioner, Health and Wellbeing, Evidence Informed Practice, Partnerships, Education, Communication, Ethical Practice, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Engagement, and Sustainability. This is a deliberately broader professional-identity framework than the typical competency checklist used by comparable clinical Masters, and it signals an intent to build practitioners who reason about role, ethics and context, not just technique.

Delivery uses a "constructive alignment" model that explicitly maps learning outcomes to teaching activities and assessment tasks, and students build an e-portfolio across the degree to document their professional journey and skills acquisition — a longitudinal, evidence-based competency record rather than a single capstone artefact. Semesters run 14 weeks on a calendar outside the standard university teaching periods specifically to accommodate clinical placement blocks, indicating the program is structured around extensive supervised, hands-on clinical practice rather than classroom-only delivery.

Graduates enter as registered physiotherapists across musculoskeletal, neurological, cardiorespiratory, and paediatric practice settings in hospitals, private practice, aged care, sports medicine, and community health. The degree is accreditation-anchored to AHPRA/Physiotherapy Board of Australia registration standards, which constrains curriculum content to nationally regulated competency domains and locks in ongoing external validation of currency.

## 2. AUTOMATION EXPOSURE PROFILE

| Task Category | Example Graduate Tasks | AI Pressure | AI Capability Today | Durability Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Manual/physical assessment | Palpation, joint mobilisation, gait analysis, manual muscle testing | LOW | Cannot replicate tactile feedback or hands-on manipulation | Requires embodied, real-time physical skill and haptic judgment no current AI system can perform |
| Hands-on treatment delivery | Manual therapy, therapeutic exercise prescription and correction, hydrotherapy | LOW | AI can suggest generic exercise plans but cannot physically execute or adjust technique in real time | Direct physical intervention on a patient's body is the core of the discipline and is not substitutable by software |
| Clinical reasoning & diagnosis | Differential diagnosis of musculoskeletal/neuro presentations, red-flag screening, treatment planning | MEDIUM | LLMs can support triage/decision-support but registered accountability and liability remain with the clinician | AHPRA registration ties legal and ethical responsibility to a human practitioner; AI can augment but not own the decision |
| Patient communication & education | Explaining diagnosis, motivating adherence, informed consent, behaviour change coaching | LOW-MEDIUM | Chatbots can deliver generic health information but lack therapeutic rapport and tailored non-verbal reading of the patient | Communication is a named curriculum element (Communication, Health and Wellbeing) and is central to therapeutic outcomes |
| Documentation & administration | Clinical notes, outcome measure recording, referral letters | HIGH | AI scribes/ambient documentation tools are already mature and rapidly displacing this task | Genuinely automatable; already commercialised in adjacent allied-health markets |
| Evidence-informed practice | Literature appraisal, applying research to individual patient case | MEDIUM | AI can summarise literature but clinical application requires contextual judgment tied to the specific patient in the room | Evidence Informed Practice is an explicit curriculum element; the risk is in the search/summary step, not the application step |
| Interprofessional & advocacy work | Case conferencing, care coordination, patient advocacy within health systems | LOW | Requires relational trust-building and system navigation across stakeholders | Advocate identity is one of the three organising pillars of the degree; not a task an AI agent can credibly perform |

**Overall Section Exposure:** LOW

**Durability Assessment:** The core value of a physiotherapist — hands-on physical assessment and treatment delivered under a regulated scope of practice — sits almost entirely outside what current or near-term AI systems can substitute. The primary automation pressure is concentrated in peripheral administrative tasks (documentation, note-taking) rather than the clinical core, and AI is far more likely to function as a documentation/decision-support augmentation than a competitor to the graduate's professional identity.

### 4. THREE THRESHOLD QUESTIONS
- **Q1:** NO — A well-prompted AI agent cannot deliver hands-on manual therapy, physical assessment, or supervised clinical treatment; it can at most draft documentation, none of which constitutes 80% of a physiotherapist's actual output.
- **Q2:** YES — The three-identity model (Self, Practitioner, Advocate) and Evidence Informed Practice element explicitly train graduates to reason, judge, and own clinical decisions rather than execute a fixed protocol.
- **Q3:** YES — As AI absorbs routine documentation and administrative burden elsewhere in health systems, the comparative value of graduates who can deliver irreplaceable hands-on clinical care and complex patient communication is likely to rise, not fall.

## 5. ANALOGUE GRADUATE PROFILE
The most exposed adjacent archetype circa 2021-2022 is not the treating physiotherapist but the **allied-health administrative/documentation support role** — clinical scribes, outcome-measure data-entry staff, and junior roles heavy in note-writing and scheduling. Tools now threatening that specific task cluster include Nuance DAX (ambient clinical documentation), Heidi Health (AI medical scribe, Australian-built and used in allied health), Abridge, Otter.ai for consult transcription, and generic LLM-based triage chatbots (e.g., early symptom-checker bots). None of these tools threaten the treating physiotherapist role itself, but they materially compress the administrative shell around it.

### 6. VERDICT
The Doctor of Physiotherapy sits solidly in the MODERATE RISK band, not because its clinical core is threatened — it is one of the most AI-resistant graduate profiles in the entire portfolio due to irreducible physical/manual skill and AHPRA-anchored accountability — but because the program has a clear, correctable gap in explicit AI literacy and governance content (D5) and lacks fully evidenced primary-research and granular outcome-tracking components (D7, D10). This is a program whose risk score is depressed by curriculum documentation gaps rather than by genuine labour-market exposure, making it one of the more straightforward MODERATE RISK programs to move into RESILIENT territory.

## 7. RECOMMENDATIONS
| Priority | Action | Dimension | Effort |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Introduce an explicit AI-in-healthcare module covering ambient scribes, AI-assisted triage, and clinical decision-support tools, including their failure modes and governance obligations under AHPRA | D5 | Medium |
| 2 | Formalise a research methods unit requiring students to design and conduct a small primary clinical study or audit, not just appraise literature | D7 | Medium |
| 3 | Publish granular graduate destination data (practice setting, region, time-to-registration-to-employment) alongside existing AHPRA registration outcomes | D10 | Low |
| 4 | Embed a documented curriculum advisory loop (industry/AHPRA/alumni feedback cycle) referencing the existing constructive-alignment framework, with a public review cadence | D9 | Low |
| 5 | Extend the e-portfolio to explicitly capture AI-tool literacy competencies alongside existing professional-identity evidence | D5, D9 | Low |
| 6 | Add a systems-level failure-mode/constraint-reasoning assessment (e.g., complex multi-morbidity case simulation) to strengthen D2 beyond current clinical-reasoning coverage | D2 | Medium |

## 8. THE REDESIGNED GRADUATE PROFILE
The 2027-ready Doctor of Physiotherapy graduate retains everything that already makes this degree durable — deep manual-therapy skill, AHPRA-accountable clinical judgment, and a strong ethical/relational practice base — while adding fluency in supervising and critiquing AI-assisted clinical tools: ambient documentation systems, AI triage support, and outcome-tracking analytics, understood well enough to catch their errors rather than defer to them.

This graduate also carries a genuine, if modest, primary-research capability, having designed and executed a small clinical audit or study rather than only appraising others' evidence, and enters the workforce backed by an institution that can point to granular, published outcome data. The result is a practitioner whose irreplaceable physical and relational skill is now paired with visible, governance-literate command of the AI layer increasingly surrounding — but never replacing — the clinical encounter itself.
