## 2. RECENT JOB AD SIGNALS

> **Confidence: LOW** — this section reflects structural market knowledge of the Australian clinical psychology/neuropsychology labour market (registration data, sector reporting, and known workforce shortages) rather than a live job-ad scrape for this session.

**Signal 1 — Clinical Neuropsychologist, public hospital rehabilitation service.** Persistent advertising for registrar and fully-endorsed neuropsychologist positions in metropolitan and regional hospital rehabilitation and aged-care services, frequently unfilled for extended periods — consistent with widely reported national psychologist workforce shortages.

**Signal 2 — Private practice neuropsychology assessment roles.** Private allied-health and psychology group practices regularly advertise for neuropsychologists able to conduct independent cognitive assessments, citing multi-month patient wait-lists as a driver of hiring need.

**Signal 3 — NDIS-linked paediatric neuropsychological assessment.** Growing advertised demand for practitioners able to conduct developmental/paediatric neuropsychological assessments tied to NDIS funding applications and school-based supports.

**Signal 4 — Medico-legal/forensic neuropsychology panels.** Law firms and insurers periodically seek panel-listed neuropsychologists for independent medico-legal assessments in personal injury and capacity matters — a durable, higher-billing niche.

**Signal 5 — Postdoctoral/academic research fellow (cognitive neuroscience, clinical psychology).** University and medical-research-institute postings for PhD-qualified researchers in ageing, TBI, and neurodegeneration research, often funded through NHMRC/MRFF grants.

**Signal 6 — Aged-care and dementia-service clinical roles.** Increasing advertised demand tied to dementia being the second-leading cause of death in Australia and continued expansion of specialist dementia and cognitive-decline services.
