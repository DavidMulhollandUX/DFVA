## 7. ASSESSMENT REDESIGN EXAMPLES
1. **AI-Assisted Documentation Audit:** Students review a set of AI-scribe-generated clinical notes for a simulated patient encounter, identify errors or omissions, and produce a corrected, defensible clinical record — directly assessing D5 governance literacy alongside existing documentation competency.
2. **Multi-Morbidity Case Simulation:** A high-fidelity simulated patient with overlapping musculoskeletal, cardiorespiratory, and cognitive presentations requires students to explicitly reason through competing treatment priorities and failure modes, defended in a structured viva — strengthening D2 and D4.
3. **Clinical Practice Audit Project:** In place of a purely literature-based Evidence Informed Practice assignment, students design and conduct a small audit of outcome-measure data from a placement site, then present findings with methodological defence — building genuine D7 primary-research capability.

---

## 8. AI GOVERNANCE AND QUALITY CONTROLS
- Require all AI-scribe or documentation-tool use during placements to be explicitly supervised and verified by a registered clinical educator, with errors logged as part of the e-portfolio evidence base.
- Embed AHPRA's Physiotherapy Board digital-health and telehealth guidance directly into the AI-in-healthcare module so governance content stays anchored to the regulator, not just vendor tools.
- Establish a standing curriculum review checkpoint (tied to the new advisory cycle) specifically scoped to reassess AI-tool relevance annually, given how fast ambient documentation tools are evolving.
- Prohibit any AI tool from being positioned as a substitute for hands-on clinical assessment or manual therapy competency sign-off — AI use is restricted to documentation, triage-support, and literature-synthesis tasks only.

## 9. MEASUREMENT PLAN
* **Leading indicators (12 months):** AI-in-healthcare module completion rate; number of students completing a primary clinical audit/research project; advisory board convened and public review published; first graduate destination snapshot published.
* **Lagging indicators (12-24 months):** DFVA re-assessment score movement from 26/36 toward 28/36+; AHPRA registration-to-employment time trend; graduate self-reported AI-tool confidence in exit surveys; placement-site feedback on graduates' AI-governance awareness.

---

## 10. RISKS, TRADE-OFFS, AND DEPENDENCIES
- **Risk:** Adding new AI-literacy and research-project content into an already placement-dense 36-month program risks curriculum overload.
  *Mitigation:* Integrate the AI-in-healthcare module into existing Evidence Informed Practice and Communication elements rather than creating a standalone credit-bearing unit, minimising timetable disruption.
- **Risk:** Publishing granular graduate outcome data depends on alumni/careers-office data infrastructure that may not currently exist at the required granularity.
  *Mitigation:* Start with AHPRA registration-linked cohort tracking (already collected for accreditation) before expanding to salary/regional detail.
- **Risk:** AI-scribe and documentation-tool vendors change rapidly; content designed today may be outdated within 2 years.
  *Mitigation:* Frame the AI module around governance principles and error-mode literacy (durable) rather than any single vendor tool (perishable), reviewed annually via the new advisory cycle.

## 11. REDESIGNED GRADUATE PROFILE (2027 READY)
The 2027-ready Doctor of Physiotherapy graduate remains defined by what already makes this degree resilient — irreplaceable manual and physical clinical skill, AHPRA-accountable judgment, and deep ethical and relational practice — while now also demonstrating fluent, governance-literate supervision of the AI tools increasingly present in allied-health workflows, a genuine primary-research track record from a completed clinical audit, and a publicly evidenced outcomes record that lets the University of Melbourne demonstrate, not merely assert, this graduate's durable employability.
