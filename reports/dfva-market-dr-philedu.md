## 2. RECENT JOB AD SIGNALS
> **Confidence: LOW** — No live job-ad scrape was performed for this report (handbook source was a single-sentence generic PhD description); the following signals are derived from structural knowledge of the Australian and international education-research labour market, not a real-time data pull. Treat as directional, not measured.

1. **Signal 1 — Postdoctoral Research Fellow (Education), Go8 university.** Increasingly requires evidence of external grant success or track record, not just thesis completion; academic hiring committees are raising the bar as PhD output volume grows relative to open positions.
2. **Signal 2 — Senior Research/Policy Officer, State Department of Education.** Government education policy roles continue to recruit research-doctorate holders, with growing emphasis on program evaluation and evidence-based policy skills over generalist analysis.
3. **Signal 3 — Learning Designer / Instructional Designer, EdTech company.** Postings increasingly list "experience directing/evaluating AI-generated learning content" as a differentiator over "content authoring" itself, reflecting AI's absorption of routine drafting tasks.
4. **Signal 4 — Education Specialist, international development/NGO.** Roles emphasise monitoring & evaluation methodology and cross-cultural stakeholder engagement, areas where AI substitution is limited by contextual and relational demands.
5. **Signal 5 — Head of Research and Innovation, independent school.** A growing role category where practitioner-researchers (teacher-PhDs) translate research into school-level practice change — a durable, leadership-anchored role.
6. **Signal 6 — Learning Analytics Researcher, university or EdTech.** A smaller but fast-growing niche rewarding education PhD graduates with strong quantitative/data-science skills, an area where the generic education PhD often underprepares candidates.
