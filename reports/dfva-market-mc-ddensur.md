## 2. RECENT JOB AD SIGNALS
> **Confidence: LOW** — This is not a live job-board scrape; it reflects structural market knowledge of the Australian/NZ dental labour market and general trends in dental-sector hiring language, not verified current postings. Treat as directional, not evidentiary.

**Signal 1 — General Dentist, Regional/Outer-Metro Practice.** Job ads for general dentists in Australia continue to emphasise chronic undersupply outside major metro centres, often bundled with relocation incentives, signing bonuses, and visa sponsorship for overseas-trained dentists — consistent with a structurally tight labour market rather than one under demand pressure from automation.

**Signal 2 — Corporate Dental Group Associate Roles.** Large corporate dental groups (e.g., Bupa Dental, Pacific Smiles-style operators) advertise associate dentist roles increasingly mentioning "AI-assisted diagnostics" or "digital imaging systems" as part of the practice's technology stack — framed as a selling point/tool for the clinician, not a role replacement.

**Signal 3 — Public Dental Health Services.** State-run dental health services (e.g., Dental Health Services Victoria) continue to post persistent vacancies for general and community dentists, reflecting an access/workforce shortage rather than a demand contraction.

**Signal 4 — Locum and Flexible-Work Dentist Roles.** Growth in locum agency postings for short-term dental cover suggests continued strong baseline demand for registered practitioners across both public and private settings.

**Signal 5 — Practice Manager/Dental Admin Roles Increasingly AI-Literate.** Adjacent non-clinical roles (practice managers, dental receptionists) increasingly list familiarity with AI-scheduling or AI-billing tools as a preferred skill — the automatable layer is administrative, not clinical.

**Signal 6 — Specialist Registrar Positions.** Ongoing demand for post-DDS specialist training places (orthodontics, oral surgery, paediatric dentistry) signals a healthy pipeline from entry-to-practice into deeper specialisation.

**Signal 7 — Rural Health Workforce Incentive Schemes.** Commonwealth and state rural dental workforce incentive programs continue to target new dental graduates specifically, reinforcing durable, geographically distributed demand for DDS-qualified practitioners.

## 5. CURRICULUM IMPLICATIONS
| # | Implication | Dimension | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| CI-1 | AI-assisted diagnostic-imaging tools (e.g., Overjet/Pearl-style caries and pathology detection) are becoming standard in general dental practice, but the curriculum shows no explicit coverage | D5 | Introduce a dedicated AI-in-dental-diagnostics module with hands-on exposure to at least one commercial AI imaging tool and its failure modes |
| CI-2 | The administrative/documentation layer (scheduling, notes, billing) is rapidly automating around graduates, freeing clinical capacity | D9 | Add brief practice-management technology literacy content so graduates enter the workforce fluent in AI-augmented practice tools, not just clinical technique |
| CI-3 | No published granular outcome data undermines the institution's ability to demonstrate the degree's already-strong labour-market durability | D10 | Partner with careers/QILT-aligned reporting to publish registration rates, practice-setting distribution, and time-to-first-position data |
| CI-4 | The compulsory research project is a strength but is not currently linked to AI-evidence evaluation skills that graduates will need to assess new clinical AI tools | D7 | Offer an optional research-project stream evaluating the evidence base of an emerging dental AI tool |
| CI-5 | Rural/regional workforce shortage signals suggest strong demand durability independent of AI trends, an under-leveraged positioning asset for the program | D6 | Highlight structural workforce-shortage data in program marketing and career-pathway advising to reinforce resilience messaging |

| CI-7 | Systems/failure-mode reasoning in treatment planning is implicit in clinical supervision but not explicitly taught or assessed as a distinct competency | D2 | Add explicit root-cause/failure-mode-analysis case studies to treatment-planning assessment rubrics |
