This document is the **deeper perspective beneath it** — the fuller pass through the iceberg the Define-Step-3 prompts call for. It was produced by two multi-agent workflows (36 agents, ~1.7M tokens): the first analysed the system across five systemic lenses and four stakeholder vantages and consolidated **16** prevailing mental models (the vision doc names 3); the second **adversarially pressure-tested** the highest-leverage beliefs and ran a generate-and-judge panel over four candidate visions. ## The one-paragraph version

The labour-market signal that should shape curriculum is structurally **severed** from the people who hold curriculum authority — it lives in careers offices, LMI vendors and employers, and dies before it ever reaches an Academic Board. Almost every actor rationally optimises against an enrolment-, compliance- or integrity-shaped metric, while *graduate-outcome durability is nobody's owned KPI* and its cost is an externality borne by students. The deep beliefs holding this in place are **durable, not flippable** in 2–5 years. So the winning move is **not** to bet on changing minds — it is to build the boundary-respecting *instrument and channel* that lets a sponsor convert these standing tensions into a defensible decision, while a rare 2026–2027 window (TEQSA's program-level reform pivot, ATEC managed-growth compacts, the OSCA taxonomy reset, the *measured* AI entry-level shock, and a 19-month gatekeeper silence from TechnologyOne) holds the door open.

## 🏗️ Systemic Structures — the six questions, answered

### What each group is trying to achieve — and the tension the *structure* produces

The tensions below are **designed-in conflicts of incentive**, not failures of will.

| Actor | What it wants | The structural tension (the addressable frustration) |
|---|---|---|
| **University leadership** (DVC Education / Provost) | Defensible, evidence-based portfolio decisions | Accountable for a feedback loop running **5–7 years** while AI restructures jobs in **12–18 months** — and now newly exposed because ATEC's managed-growth shift (2026→27) **removes the "just enrol more" escape valve**, making each funded place's viability a sharper question than ever. They carry anecdote into a board conversation that demands evidence. |
| **Program directors** | Meet outcomes, manage accreditation | The actor with the *appetite and duty* to act has **"little if any formal power,"** ~**200 hrs** of revision load per change, and a research-first promotion culture that ignores it. They are the **USER** of a curriculum tool but almost never the **BUYER** — the single biggest adoption risk. |
| **Faculty** | Teach the discipline; (voluntarily) prepare students | Their strongest AI-adoption driver is the *obligation to prepare students*, yet teaching/AI capability is rewarded nowhere in promotion; any tool reading as "an algorithm grading my discipline" triggers resistance, policed by the **NTEU line** (support OK; replacement = dispute) being re-fought in 2026 bargaining. |
| **Students** | Career outcomes on a $40k–200k+ bet | Bear **almost the entire cost** of misalignment while holding the **least power** to influence it; choose on backward-looking proxies. The destroyed value appears on no institution's P&L — *the externality at the centre of the system*. |
| **Employers** | A pipeline of work-ready, AI-ready hires | Rank technical skills **first**; educators rank them **last**. Only 28% think HE meets expectations (vs 78% of HE leaders). Formal channels (advisory boards) are non-binding and lagged; real influence travels through invisible personal relationships. |
| **Accreditors / regulators** | Learning assurance, award integrity | A **deliberate scope boundary at assessment integrity** leaves curriculum-market durability ungoverned — the *complementarity gap* (TEQSA does integrity; ACSES does principles; **neither provides an operational methodology**). A degree can be fully accredited and producing under-prepared graduates. |
| **CMS vendors** (TechnologyOne/CourseLoop, Akari) | Workflow lock-in; "course design → graduation" | The actor **best placed** to close the gap is **least motivated**: 19+ months post-acquisition, zero LMI partner, zero outcomes module. The gatekeeper is standing still. |
| **LMI vendors** (Lightcast, Faethm/Pearson) | Grow buyers; protect the data moat | Produce the exact signal curriculum needs but are **structurally repelled** from HE: a single AU university is worth far less than a corporate client, and their buyer (careers/IR) holds no curriculum authority. Skillabi stays out of ANZ; Faethm has retreated. |
| **Government funders** (ATEC / JSA) | A workforce-responsive HE system | Funds **places, not outcomes**. ATEC reshapes but doesn't remove the volume incentive, and Job-ready Graduates proved blunt price signals **fail** (Society & Culture enrolments fell only ~3pp despite a 113% fee rise). |

**The three patterns underneath:** (1) signal-holders are **organisationally severed** from curriculum authority; (2) everyone optimises against enrolment/compliance/integrity while durability is **nobody's KPI**; (3) the actor with most **authority** (DVC) is most starved of tools, while those with most **appetite** (program directors, careers teams, motivated faculty) have neither budget nor governance power.

### Where is value "left on the table"?

The **severed signal itself** (LMI exists at the far end of the chain, never reaches the curriculum decision) · the **invisible employer channel** (real influence trapped in un-scalable personal relationships) · the **misalignment externality** (value destroyed lands on students, on no institution's P&L) · **anticipatory accreditation evidence** (AACSB 4.1 / AMC live today; most bodies' AI criteria compulsory 2027–31; institutions need frameworks now) · **wasted redesign labour** (~200 hrs per change through a "cumbersome, inflexible, lengthy" process).

### Where does power concentrate?

- **TechnologyOne (the gatekeeper):** CourseLoop (dominant CMS, 11+ unis) + **60%+ SIS** + 75% timetabling + a stated "course-design-to-graduation" ambition — the most consequential infrastructure relationship *and* the most important latent competitor.
- **Data incumbents — but the moat is dissolving:** Lightcast (18bn+ data points) has **open-sourced** its 34k-skill taxonomy and the ABS replaced ANZSCO with skills-based **OSCA** (Dec 2024). The data/ontology layers are being deliberately **commoditised** to drive demand for paid interpretation — owning raw data now confers little defensibility.
- **The accreditation cartel:** statutorily protected, ~$100M+/yr fees, 4–6-yr cycles, no new entry — sell *into* it, don't enter it.
- **The academy (over content):** Australian universities **self-accredit** with no government sign-off on curriculum content — authority resides inside the academy *by law*, fortified by NTEU agreements.

### Where is the market **stagnant**? *(red-flag zones)*

Curriculum-management software (oligopoly ≈68% US; CourseLeaf shipped zero AI in 30 years; Coursedog burned ~$113M for ~$8.5M ARR) · accreditation/QA bodies (cartel, not binding until 2027–31) · government price-signal levers (*demonstrated failure*; ~$4B stripped since 2021) · internal T&L centres as a channel (defunded, shrinking, ~70% of unis in deficit).

### Where is the market **dynamic**? *(positive-signal zones)*

LMI platforms extending *up* into scoring (Lightcast/KKR: 4–5 acquisitions in 2025 + Skillabi; Faethm in Microsoft/IBM deals) · AI-in-education startups (but **83%** of capital went to the *student learning loop*, **not** the curriculum-decision niche → contestable, not crowded) · CMS vendors buying *into* intelligence (Providence→Modern Campus; Coursedog→ClassRanked) · the regulator drifting toward outcomes (**TEQSA now collects Graduate Outcomes Survey data directly from the 2025 data year**).

### Control points for innovation

The decisive insight: **the lower layers are being commoditised, so durable control sits higher up.**

1. **A governance-legible "AI-durability" STANDARD / certification mark** — the curriculum equivalent of NABERS / a Health Star Rating / B-Corp, defended by *legitimacy and citation* not data scale. **The single most defensible point**, and one a small Australian team can actually win.
2. **The cross-institutional benchmark dataset** — the *only* true data-network-effect moat, because it is the **output** data (durability scores), not the commoditised input. Path-dependent, first-mover-sensitive, cannot be retroactively manufactured. Couples with the standard into a flywheel.
3. **The embedded position at the curriculum-approval decision-moment** (the US Mapademics-in-Coursedog playbook) — gated by two human gatekeepers (the careers↔committee org split, the NTEU line) that *only service design, not code, can pass*.
4. **The LMI→curriculum integration-broker layer** for the ANZSCO/OSCA world — a tollbooth between fragmented signal and the curriculum system of record.

## 💡 Innovation Opportunity Points

**Red flags (proceed with caution):** competing as another **workflow platform** (commoditised; capital war vs PE-backed incumbents) · becoming a **raw data vendor** (you cannot out-data Lightcast/Faethm) · relying on **T&L centres/grants** (contracting buyer) · **single-market dependence** during a sector contraction · positioning the scoring as **decision-replacement** (NTEU/EA friction).

**Positive signals (real defensibility):** a proprietary **10-dimension durability methodology** that becomes a *cited standard* (uncopyable by feature-cloning) · an **Australia/ANZ regulatory-context corpus** (every competitor is US-centric) · a **decision layer above commoditised workflow** (URL-based, no-SIS, near-zero marginal cost, value-based pricing $15–35k ASP vs incumbents' $40–100k + implementation) · a **regulatory tailwind on a known schedule** (TEQSA direct GOS collection; 2027–31 accreditation wave) · **whitespace neither pincer occupies** (~18–36-month claim window).

## 🧠 Mental Models — *why* the market looks this way (and what is actually movable)

The [systems-thinking analysis](compass-systems-thinking.md) and [vision](compass-vision.md) name **3** mental models. The workflow consolidated **16**, then adversarially tested the top six. **The central finding is sobering and clarifying: the highest-"leverage" beliefs are the most durable.** Every top model came back `shift = weak`; leverage was downgraded from *high* to *medium/low*. The strategy that follows is therefore **work-with-the-belief, not flip-the-belief** — which is precisely how [compass-vision.md](compass-vision.md) already frames it ("change the system not by arguing with the belief, but by making the hidden information visible at the moment of choice").

### The top beliefs after adversarial verification

| # | Mental model | Consolidation | **Verified** | Honest implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Curriculum is the academy's inviolable domain** | high / rank 1 | shift **weak**, lev. **medium** | Legally codified (self-accreditation), NTEU-enforced, *re-entrenching* in 2026. **"Navigable, not movable."** Build the strictly-decision-support, peer-sponsored, service-designed hand-off that lives *with* it. |
| 2 | **Accreditation compliance = quality** | high / rank 2 | shift **weak**, lev. **medium** | Durability is "certified quality" by *no one*; the no-causal-evidence base bars an accreditor from adopting a forward prediction. Realistic opening: COMPASS as **anticipatory CIR/AMC evidence** on the AI-literacy axis (gated to 2027–31). |
| 3 | **Enrolment demand = value** | high / rank 3 | shift **weak**, lev. **medium** | High ceiling, **low velocity**. Leadership must *volunteer* an inference ATEC doesn't yet force → the venture must **manufacture the trigger** (austerity triage + compact evidence as portfolio risk), not ride a shift underway. |
| 4 | **Durability is below the governance line** (nobody's KPI) | high / rank 4 | shift **weak**, lev. **medium** | Usable only via a back door: *supply the missing instrument* so a sponsoring DVC/Chancellor can **manufacture the risk-register line**. Gated behind belief #1. |
| 5 | **The LMI↔curriculum link is nobody's job** | high / rank 5 | shift **weak**, lev. **LOW** | A **stable, open gap** whose only movement would be *against* COMPASS (incumbent encroachment). Value = a **time-boxed land-grab** on speed-to-sponsor, not a belief whose change creates pull. |
| 6 | **Data is the moat; interpretation is thin** | high / rank 6 | shift **moderate**, lev. **LOW** | The erosion *cuts against* the easy read: Skillabi/Faethm show incumbents **climbing into interpretation**, so "interpretation is unguarded whitespace" is *false*. A thesis-consistency check, not a lever. |

### How to *use* the 16 models — grouped by play

- **Navigate (durable demand-side beliefs):** curriculum-is-academy's-domain · durability-is-nobody's-KPI · accreditation=quality · enrolment=value · slow-deliberation=rigour. → Don't flip; build boundary-respecting, sponsor-carried artefacts.
- **Exploit *now* (open but time-boxed supply-side windows):** lmi-curriculum-link-is-not-our-job · data-is-the-moat. → Move fast; these can close *against* you.
- **Framing prerequisites (adoption mechanics):** employer-gap-is-soft-skills (re-anchor to the *exposure* axis) · education-is-non-instrumental-formation (position as *necessary-but-not-sufficient*) · knowledge-validated-by-peers (be *sponsored* by a peer, not *sold*).
- **High ceiling / low feasibility — do NOT bet on these:** degree-is-brand-insurance · teaching-is-career-neutral-service · degree-replaced-by-skills-hiring · rankings-are-the-quality-proxy. *(Job-ready Graduates proves student-facing repricing won't happen on this horizon.)*

## 👀 Vision — corroborated, and sharpened

The judge panel was **unanimous** (all three judges) and all independently proposed the same blend. Critically, the result **converges on the vision already written in [compass-vision.md](compass-vision.md)** — independent triangulation from 36 agents that the heading is sound. The blended statement adds three things to the existing one: a **measurable bright-line**, an explicit **legibility device**, and a **returning-cost loop**.

> **By 2031, a program's durability against an AI-reshaped labour market is a standing, evidence-based input that every curriculum review must see — and act on within a single governance cycle — so that *content currency*, like assessment integrity and accreditation today, becomes a governed and publicly legible property of a degree, with an owner who bears the cost of letting it lapse.**
>
> A curriculum not re-read against the labour market within the year is treated as **unverified, not merely slow**. Durability is a *named, transparent property* a board, accreditor, employer or student can read at the program level — the way a building carries an energy rating. And because it is visible and owned, the cost of a misaligned graduate stops being an invisible private loss and becomes a **reputational signal that returns to the institution that designed the program.**

**On the horizon (a genuine founder's call).** [compass-vision.md](compass-vision.md) reasons to **seven years (~2033)** and flags five years as "bolder and riskier… implies regulatory or peak-body adoption." The workflow panel leaned **five years (~2031)** *specifically because* the blend is bounded to ride only forces already in motion and needs **neither** of the two lowest-probability flips (students repricing degrees in real time; rankings methodologies changing). Both are defensible; the difference is a dial on how hard the roadmap chases regulators/peak bodies vs early-mover institutions. Recommendation: keep the **7-year concrete** statement as the internal compass and the directional one-liner for pitches (per the existing doc), and treat the **2031** read as the aggressive-but-evidenced scenario that the 2026–27 TEQSA/ATEC/OSCA window makes plausible.

**Why a vision (not a solution) matters.** Built only for *today's* obvious opportunity — "sell a program-alignment score" — COMPASS competes with Lightcast Skillabi on the incumbent's axis. Aimed at where the system is *heading* — content currency as a governed, legible property — it can be the player who saw it was possible first, and **survive any number of pivots** (a standard, a benchmark, an integration broker, an accreditation-evidence engine are all paths to the same future).

**What the vision leverages.** The **severance** of signal from authority · the **pre-commoditised, unserved** AU niche · **TEQSA's 2026 program-level pivot** · the **measured AI entry-level shock** (Stanford Nov 2025: 16% relative employment fall for AI-exposed 22–25s) · employer vocabulary moving from "soft skills" to **"AI exposure"** · **ATEC mission-based compacts** · the **OSCA taxonomy reset** (free, authoritative, AI-cognisant; leaves foreign incumbents ANZSCO-lagged) · the proven **Mapademics-in-Coursedog** precedent · and the **NTEU decision-support line** — the constraint it rides within, which is *also* what makes it feasible.

*Candidate-vision scores (judge panel, /25): Labour-market integration **23.5** ★ · Learner/graduate-outcome **22** · Curriculum agility **21.5** · Trust & certification **20.75**. The latter three were folded in as layers, not discarded.*

### Traps to avoid

Don't compete as a **workflow platform** · don't become a **raw data vendor** · don't depend on **T&L centres/grants** · don't position as **decision-replacement** (NTEU) · don't position **against the degree** (the "degree is dead" market is a data-contradicted mirage — *re-substantiate* it instead) · don't build a **student-comparison product** (JRG proved it doesn't change behaviour) · don't **bet on flipping deep academy beliefs** in 2–5 years.

## The single biggest risk

The vision, the standard (P4) and the credibility of every durability score rest on the evidence base linking curriculum to outcomes. Closing this gap — instrumenting from day one to link COMPASS scores to QILT graduate outcomes over time, moving from *predicted* to *validated* durability — is simultaneously the venture's **largest credibility risk and its most defensible long-term moat.** It should be a first-class design goal.

> **Update (EXP-04, 2026-06-05 — see [compass-exp04-causal-evidence.md](compass-exp04-causal-evidence.md)).** The earlier framing — "*no* causal evidence exists" — was **overstated and is retired.** A systematic search found credible difference-in-differences evidence (Salomons et al. 2025, German VET; Swiss/Dutch corroboration) that demand-aligned curriculum *revision causally raises graduate wages*. This **shrinks the risk and strengthens the "why now"** — the mechanism is now evidenced. A **narrower, still-ownable moat survives**: no study validates a *scored durability instrument*, against a *commercial LMI signal*, in *higher education*, linked to *QILT* outcomes, in *Australia* — which COMPASS can be first to produce via the staged PROSPECT-DiD protocol in that doc. The sharper open question to carry into interviews: the causal evidence comes from *legally-binding, coordinated* VET curricula, so does the payoff survive in Australia's *autonomous, self-accrediting* universities?

## Where this leaves us (the loop continues)

This deepens [compass-vision.md](compass-vision.md) without replacing it — and it sharpens the two load-bearing assumptions the vision already names. Take into expert conversations: (1) **Demand** — will a DVC/strategy buyer *volunteer* the demand≠durability inference (P2, P6) the funding system doesn't yet force? (2) **Trust & standing** — can the independent durability standard (P4) earn enough authority to be *expected* before an incumbent occupies the adjacency (P9)? The adversarial verification says the deep beliefs won't flip on their own — so the venture's whole game is to be the boundary-respecting instrument that lets a sponsor act *within* them, fast, while the 2026–27 window holds.
