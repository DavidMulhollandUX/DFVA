## 4. Strategic Implications

1. **AI Literacy is the universal gap.** Every program could add 1-2 points by incorporating AI literacy into core curriculum. This alone would push 7 programs into RESILIENT territory.

2. **Clinical/health programs are structurally resilient.** Programs with clinical placements, regulatory accreditation, and hands-on patient care (psychology, physiotherapy, dentistry, nursing) cluster at 23-27. Their AI defence is regulatory and practical, not curricular.

3. **Business programs are bimodal.** MC-APBUSA (20) has exceptional AI literacy but high automation exposure. MC-BAMKTG (17) and MC-BASE (18) combine high exposure with weak technical depth. The business faculty has both the biggest AI opportunity and the biggest AI risk.

4. **Market data transformed D10.** Before QILT enrichment, virtually every program scored 1 on Outcome Evidence. After enrichment with real employment and salary data, most programs now score 2-3. This demonstrates that the gap was data availability, not poor outcomes.

5. **Creative arts is the canary.** MC-SCWR at 11/36 CRITICAL reflects a genuine structural threat to creative practice programs. The program's all-compulsory, craft-focused design offers no AI defence. Other creative programs (journalism, fine arts, music) likely face similar pressure.
