## 🔍 Part A — The systematic search

### Method

Six parallel angles: (1) CourseLoop product pages, release notes, roadmap webinars; (2) TNE (ASX:TNE) investor & strategy communications; (3) hiring signals (a leading indicator of roadmap); (4) partnerships / data-licences with skills-data vendors; (5) the Brian Clark / CourseLoop-product-lead public footprint + HE-sector commentary; (6) the adjacent §1.5 factual questions. Then **three adversarial steelman passes** explicitly tasked to *prove the roadmap item exists* (official-signals lens, leading-indicators lens, market-pressure lens) — so that a null is robust precisely because motivated searchers could not break it.

### The strongest "presence" case — and why every brick failed

After being told to build the strongest possible case that TNE *is* building this, **no brick survived scrutiny as a scoped/contracted commitment**:

1. **National Skills Passport op-eds by a named TNE exec.** Peter Nikoletatos (Industry GM Education): *"A regulated taxonomy is critical… the National Skills Passport can set a global benchmark"*; skills passport as *"'LinkedIn skills' but on steroids and validated"* (technology1.com, 24 May 2024 & 31 Jan 2025). — **Fails:** advocacy for a *federal-government* initiative built on My eQuals; the lone product sentence names **OneEducation / DxP, not CourseLoop**, and never mentions curriculum-to-skills mapping. ~19 months old, no conversion.
2. **Acquisition / investor demand-side language.** Ed Chung: CourseLoop gives *"data-driven insights … to create courses that the market demands"* and addresses *"an increased focus on graduate outcomes"* (4 Nov 2024). — **Fails:** "market demands" = student-enrolment demand + program financial viability; "graduate outcomes" is named as a sector *headwind*, never as an LMI capability CourseLoop would ship.
3. **A product substrate exists.** Curriculum Mapper ships an **Insights Heatmap** and **Credit Match**. — **Fails:** the Heatmap maps only *internal* constructs (standards, accreditation, learning outcomes) and flags curriculum "overlaps/gaps"; no external taxonomy, no jobs data, no AI.
4. **Latent motive + tooling.** A forward-dated "OneEducation 2030 vision"; horizontal agentic AI (Plus/Guide, with a Data & AI hire 20 May 2026); a "New TechOne" reset (5 Mar 2026). — **Fails:** all adjacency/capability, not commitment — and each was a prime venue to announce a skills/LMI direction and was *silent* on it.

### The confirmations of absence — at the moments a real signal would have to appear

- **The single most authoritative artifact is clean.** CourseLoop **26.1 Certified Release (16 Mar 2026)** — read in full — covers accessibility, Product API v1.9, Publisher CDN/WAF, requisite rules, the Insights Heatmap (secure-assessment indicator only), Credit Match UX. **Zero** mention of skills taxonomy, LMI, graduate outcomes, employability, occupations, ESCO/O*NET/Lightcast, or AI. The release index back through 24.x has the same internal-curriculum character.
- **The most recent exec/investor record points elsewhere.** **H1 FY26 results + earnings call (19 May 2026):** AI = "Plus, Guide and in-product AI," framed as *horizontal* ERP expanding a $13.5bn TAM; the only dated education release is **Guide for students (Aug 2026)**, a student-services chatbot that Times Higher Education (24 Feb 2026) confirms does **not** touch curriculum/skills/LMI/graduate outcomes. CourseLoop is named only as generic "new IP."
- **No hard leading indicators.** **Trademarks:** IP Australia shows only SAAS+, DXP, APP BUILDER (2023-era) — zero skills/curriculum/CourseLoop marks 2024–26. **Hiring:** 28 TNE LinkedIn roles + Seek sweeps (last 6 months) — no Skills Ontology / Labour-Market Data Scientist / Graduate-Outcomes Analyst; only a *horizontal* Data & AI role. **Partnerships:** none with Lightcast, Faethm-Pearson, Jobs & Skills Australia (OSCA) or Mapademics; Lightcast's own partner page omits TNE/CourseLoop. **Analysts:** FNArena (May & Nov 2025) treat CourseLoop as a ~$9.1m ARR cross-sell add-on, with zero skills/LMI discussion.
- **Even adjacent customer skills work is decoupled.** The UNSW ADA Skills Taxonomy / Skills Passport white paper (Nov 2025), at a CourseLoop customer, does not name CourseLoop or TNE as its engine.

> ⚠️ **Flagged false-positive (do not be fooled on re-run).** A Perplexity query injected fabricated "sources" claiming a `technologyone.com.au/products/courseloop` LMI page ("50,000+ skills taxonomy") and a "CourseLoop 2026 Product Roadmap" press release dated 15 Nov 2025. **These are hallucinations** — the real domain is technology1.com, the URLs do not resolve, and they appear in no genuine source list. Excluded from the verdict.

## 🧭 Part B — The verdict against the decision rule

**The kill-signal is NOT tripped.** A "scoped/contracted" signal requires a hard artifact — a release-note line, a dated roadmap commitment, a skills-data partnership/licence, a skills-themed trademark, a skills/LMI job req, or an exec statement naming the capability. **None exists.** Therefore, per the backlog decision rule:

- EXP-02's core assumption — *the LMI/skills-demand/graduate-outcomes layer above the curriculum system-of-record is currently vacant* — **survives.** Do **not** kill or deprioritise on incumbent-roadmap grounds.
- The backlog's "if EXP-02 fails → pivot-within to partner/acquire posture, fast" branch is **not** triggered. **Wave-1 outreach proceeds in its current independent-standard framing.**

The honest, narrower statement to carry across the workbook:

> ~~"TechnologyOne will close the Stage-3→4 gap."~~
> **"As of mid-2026 the gap is demonstrably still open — TechnologyOne has the motive and the system-of-record but has shipped no LMI/skills/graduate-outcomes capability and signalled none. The window is real but not guaranteed; the most probable closer is a fast-follow *partnership* (the Coursedog × Mapademics pattern), not an in-house build."**

## 📡 Part C — The monitoring protocol (because a desk sweep can't close a non-public roadmap)

A desk experiment proves *public* absence; it cannot prove a private roadmap is empty. Two follow-throughs close the residual risk.

**C1 · The verbal half — EXP-02 interview (#16 Brian Clark, GM Curriculum, TechnologyOne).** Carry the backlog probe, now sharpened by this desk read: *"Is LMI / skills-demand integration into CourseLoop on the 2026 roadmap — and if not, what's the stated position on it as a feature?"* → *"Coursedog shipped exactly this via Mapademics in May. If a third party built the durability layer on top of CourseLoop, partner or threat?"* Fast-track via UniMelb's existing TechnologyOne relationship.

**C2 · The tripwires — re-check before any irreversible bet** (cleanest, currently-empty signals first):

1. **A skills-data partnership / data-licence** (Lightcast, Mapademics, Jobs & Skills Australia/OSCA, Faethm-Pearson) — the "buy don't build" tell. **Most likely first signal**, because **Coursedog shipped LMI-in-workflow via Mapademics on 22 May 2026**, creating a direct forcing function. Watch too for **Lightcast Skillabi launching in ANZ** (today US/Canada/UK only).
2. **A skills/passport/outcomes-themed trademark** filed by TNE at IP Australia (today zero) — the cleanest objective tripwire.
3. **A skills/LMI-specific job req** ("Skills Ontology Lead," "Labour-Market Data Scientist," "Graduate-Outcomes Analyst") at TNE/CourseLoop.
4. **A future CourseLoop release note (post-26.1)** or product page adding an external skills framework (ESCO/O*NET/OSCA), labour-market data, or graduate-outcome analytics.
5. **A CourseLoop-specific AI announcement** tying Plus / in-product AI to curriculum intelligence or employer-demand/graduate-outcome prediction (vs the horizontal Guide).
6. **OneEducation 2030 vision** gaining an explicit skills/employability/LMI workstream.
7. **A soft LinkedIn signal** from Brian Clark (GM Curriculum) or Carolyn McInnis (Head of Product) — the most likely earliest place an informal "skills is coming" hint surfaces; both warrant a manual logged-in pass.

## 🧩 Adjacent questions resolved (research-plan §1.5)

**Q1 · Which AU/NZ universities hold a Lightcast "Labour Insight" subscription beyond UoM?** **No second AU/NZ *university* is publicly nameable** — an evidence gap (private B2B subscriptions), not confirmed absence. Lightcast markets "Labour Insight (ANZ)" to higher ed and names non-university ANZ clients (the former National Skills Commission → now JSA; TAFE NSW; NZ's Tokona te Raki). The only named ANZ *university* case study is **Melbourne itself**. Deakin shows an association (a Lightcast webinar) but no confirmed licence; Macquarie uses **LinkUp**, not Lightcast. *Confidence: moderate-high on the conclusion, low on completeness. Best route to names: AusTender / state procurement portals.*

**Q2 · Has any Australian university used Faethm (by Pearson) for curriculum alignment?** **Qualified yes — Deakin, via DeakinCo.** Faethm's Higher Education factsheet lists curriculum use-cases and names **Deakin** (testimonial from the DeakinCo CEO). **Honesty flag:** DeakinCo builds *professional short courses / micro-credentials*, not accredited degree curriculum — between workforce consulting and academic course design. **No public, named example of an AU university using Faethm to redesign accredited degree curricula.** *Confidence: moderate (vendor-marketing sources).*

**Q3 · Which curriculum-management system does UoM use?** **Not CourseLoop (and not Kuali/Coursedog).** UoM runs an in-house stack — **CAPS (Curriculum Approval and Publication System)** + **StudentOne** — evidenced by the Handbook's "CAPS Login – Staff only" and UoM Curriculum Officer role descriptions. CourseLoop does **not** list UoM among its ~10 AU university clients (UNSW, UTS, Wollongong, Monash, QUT, Murdoch, Macquarie, Flinders, Western Sydney, Charles Sturt, UniSQ). *Confidence: high that UoM does NOT use CourseLoop; moderate on whether CAPS is fully bespoke vs vendor-underpinned.*

> **Cross-cutting:** Lightcast's *curriculum-to-skills* product (Skillabi, ~33k-skill taxonomy) is **not yet sold in ANZ** — in the home market Lightcast sells only raw Labour Insight. That product gap is directly favourable to COMPASS's positioning.

## 🎯 Implications for the venture

1. **H4 holds — proceed, don't pivot.** The Stage-3→4 adjacency is demonstrably still open as of mid-2026. Wave-1 outreach keeps its independent-standard framing; the partner/acquire branch stays holstered.
2. **Speed is now an evidenced constraint, not a vibe.** The real forcing function isn't TNE's silence — it's **Coursedog × Mapademics shipping LMI-in-workflow (22 May 2026)**. The incumbent path most likely runs through a *fast-follow partnership*, which can appear with little warning. The "why now" is sharper, and the bet is genuinely time-boxed.
3. **UoM is not a CourseLoop shop — this strengthens the inside-the-buyer thesis.** COMPASS at UoM isn't displacing a CourseLoop deployment; it sits beside an in-house CAPS+StudentOne stack. The Australian curriculum-SoR landscape is fragmented, which favours an SoR-agnostic durability layer over one bolted to a single vendor — carry this into the EXP-10 data-flow interviews (#3 Careers, #4 IR).
4. **The home-market product gap is real.** With Skillabi absent from ANZ and no incumbent shipping curriculum-to-labour-market mapping locally, the category COMPASS is entering is genuinely unoccupied *here* — reinforcing the "why here" for the EXP-03 standing interviews.
5. **Don't desk-close it.** This is *public* absence, not proof. The cheapest closing moves are the #16 Brian Clark conversation (C1) and standing monitoring of the seven tripwires (C2) — especially any TNE skills-data partnership.
