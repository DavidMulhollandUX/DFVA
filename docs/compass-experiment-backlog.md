# COMPASS — Prioritised Experiment Backlog

**The 12 problems/levers turned into falsifiable experiments, mapped to the Validate-pack interviews**

**Prepared by:** David Mulholland, Associate Director, Service Experience & Design, University of Melbourne
**Date:** 2026-06-05
**Builds on:** [Deeper Perspective](compass-deeper-perspective.md) (the 12 problems/levers P1–P12) · [Validate](compass-validate.md) (hypotheses H1–H4, the 22-target list, interview guides A–E) · [Research Plan](research-plan.md) (the 8 falsifiable questions FQ1–8)

---

> **What this is.** [compass-deeper-perspective.md](compass-deeper-perspective.md) produced 12 levers of change. [compass-validate.md](compass-validate.md) produced 22 interview targets and 4 hypotheses. This document **welds the two together**: each lever becomes a falsifiable *experiment* with a method, a mapped interviewee, and an explicit kill-signal — then the whole set is **prioritised** so the riskiest, most time-sensitive bets are tested first. It keeps the Validate discipline (*aim to be proven wrong*) and adds one thing the verbal guides lack: **artifact-reaction tests** — show a real COMPASS output and watch what they *do*, which is a far stronger demand signal than "would you want this?".

### How priority was set

Three factors, in order: **(1) existential risk** — does failure reshape or kill the venture? (the load-bearing H1/H2 outrank H3/H4, which outrank the moats); **(2) time-sensitivity** — H4/the gatekeeper and the OSCA window can close *against* us at any moment; **(3) cost-to-test** — warm internal interviews are cheap and fast, cold vendor/regulator ones are slow. The result is three tiers:

- **P0 — settle first:** load-bearing (H1 *or* H2) **or** time-sensitive. Failure changes what we build.
- **P1 — de-risk the model:** the conditions that make the load-bearing assumptions *operational* (who pays, the union line, user pull, felt urgency).
- **P2 — sharpen the moat:** matters for *how* we win, not *whether*. Run once P0/P1 look green.

## 🧪 The experiments

### Tier P0 — settle first

**EXP-01 · Independent-score acceptance test** — *the H1 core*
- **Levers:** P2 (un-instrumented accountability), P3 (user/buyer split), P5 (durability-is-nobody's-KPI). · **Hyp:** H1 (demand). · **FQ1.**
- **Riskiest assumption:** decision-makers genuinely *want* a hard external durability score they can be held to. We're wrong if the appetite is rhetorical ("we'd never let an outside number grade our degrees").
- **Method:** Guide A interview **+ artifact-reaction test** — put a real COMPASS portfolio read in front of them (validated scores e.g. **B-DES 17/36, MC-IS 18/36**) and watch what they *do*. Targets: #1 Council member, #2 Director T&L, #9/#10 DVCs.
- **Probe:** *"Here is your portfolio scored on AI-era durability. What's your first instinct — and what would you do with it on Monday?"* → then: *"Would you let an independent number grade your degrees' durability? What would make that intolerable?"*
- ✅ **Survives if:** unprompted move toward a decision ("I'd take this to the portfolio committee"), asks to see more programs, or names a budget. ❌ **Killed if:** deflects to accreditation ("we're compliant, so we're fine"), or rejects external scoring on principle.
- **If it fails →** H1 fails — the Vision's central bet fails; loop back to a different framing (e.g. faculty-facing only, or careers-facing).

**EXP-02 · Gatekeeper-closing test** — *the time-sensitive wildcard* ⏱
- **Levers:** P9 (TechnologyOne 19-month silence), P1 (severed-signal connector), P8 (OSCA-native). · **Hyp:** H4 (unoccupied adjacency). · **FQ2.**
- **Riskiest assumption:** the Stage-3→4 gap is genuinely *unoccupied* and stays open long enough to establish standing. We're wrong if TechnologyOne has scoped/contracted LMI into CourseLoop's 2026 roadmap.
- **Method:** Guide D. **Single most consequential conversation = #16 Brian Clark (GM Curriculum, TechnologyOne).** Corroborate with #17 Lightcast ANZ, #18 Akari.
- **Probe:** *"Is LMI integration into CourseLoop on the 2026 roadmap — and if not, what's the stated position on it as a feature?"* → *"If a third party built the durability layer on top of CourseLoop, partner or threat?"*
- ✅ **Survives if:** no LMI on the 2026 roadmap **and** a "we'd partner, not build" posture. ❌ **Killed if:** LMI integration is scoped/contracted → the wedge disappears; pivot immediately to partner/acquire-target positioning.
- **Why now, not Wave 3:** it's a cold target but **gates the entire speed-to-sponsor thesis**. **Recommendation: fast-track via UniMelb's existing TechnologyOne relationship** rather than waiting for Wave 3.

**EXP-03 · Standing test** — *the H2 core*
- **Levers:** P4 (own the durability standard), P5, P7 (regulatory window). · **Hyp:** H2 (trust & standing). · **FQ7.**
- **Riskiest assumption:** an *independent* layer can earn enough authority to become *expected* (the Health Star / B-Corp / Moody's path). We're wrong if standing can only come from a regulator or peak body and a startup score is ignored.
- **Method:** Guide E. Targets: #8 CSHE, #7 LH Martin (credibility check), #15 ACSES authors (Principle 6 *is* curriculum currency — the ideal challenge interview), #19 AACSB, #21 TEQSA-adjacent.
- **Probe:** *"Could an independent durability signal ever carry enough authority to be expected — or does that standing only come from a regulator/peak body? What would it take for a body like yours to reference or co-own it?"*
- ✅ **Survives if:** a credible insider says an independent standard *could* be cited/referenced, and the precedent logic lands. ❌ **Killed if:** consensus that only TEQSA/a peak body can confer standing and a startup signal would be distrusted.
- **If it fails →** H2 fails — pivot the model from *independent standard* toward *partner-with/feed-a-peak-body* (co-own with AACSB/ACSES) as the route to standing.

**EXP-04 · Causal-credibility foundation** — *the empty category*
- **Lever:** P12 (no causal evidence links LMI-informed redesign to outcomes). · **Hyp:** H2 (underpins all credibility).
- **Riskiest assumption:** the durability prediction can be made *defensible enough* to act on. We're wrong if experts say a forward prediction with no causal base is structurally unfalsifiable and can never be evidence.
- **Method:** **Desk experiment** (systematic search for *any* peer-reviewed causal LMI→graduate-outcome evidence; design the COMPASS-score → QILT-outcome longitudinal measurement protocol) **+** validate the design with HE researchers #7 LH Martin, #8 CSHE.
- **Probe (to researchers):** *"If we instrumented to link durability scores to QILT outcomes over time, would that be methodologically defensible — or is the prediction unfalsifiable in principle?"*
- ✅ **Survives if:** a credible measurement pathway exists and researchers say "instrument that and it'd hold." ❌ **Killed if:** the prediction is judged unfalsifiable → the standard can never be evidence (re-scope to *decision-support framing* only, not *certified durability*).
- **Note:** this is simultaneously the **biggest credibility risk and the highest-value long-term moat** — own the first evidence base.
- ✅ **RUN → [compass-exp04-causal-evidence.md](compass-exp04-causal-evidence.md).** Result: the strict "empty category" is **retired** (demand-aligned curriculum revision is now causally evidenced to raise wages — Salomons et al. 2025, German VET DiD), but a **narrower instrument-validation moat survives** (no scored-instrument / HE / QILT / Australian study exists). The desk experiment produced the staged **PROSPECT-DiD** measurement protocol (pre-register + freeze scores → back-test predictive validity on UoM's own GOS files in <1 yr → DiD matures). Net: the risk shrank and the "why now" strengthened.

### Tier P1 — de-risk the model

**EXP-05 · User burden & trust test** — Guide B · *H1 (user side)*
- **Levers:** P3 (200-hr redesign burden), P11 (decision-support/peer-validation). · **FQ8.**
- **Riskiest assumption:** a generated redesign roadmap creates genuine pull from program directors, *and* they'll trust (not reject) external skills data.
- **Method:** Guide B **+ artifact:** show a sample discipline-specific redesign roadmap. Targets: #6 Program Director (just-reviewed), #5 academic developer.
- **Probe:** *"Had someone handed you this roadmap during your review, would it have saved time — or would you have trusted, reframed, or rejected it?"*
- ✅ **Survives if:** "I'd have wanted this"; treats it as a credible input. ❌ **Killed if:** rejected as managerial encroachment / category error → leans on the EXP-06 NTEU constraint; reframe as peer-sponsored.

**EXP-06 · NTEU feasibility-line test** — Guide E · *the named headwind*
- **Lever:** P11. · **FQ5.**
- **Riskiest assumption:** COMPASS's framing stays safely on the decision-*support* side of the line.
- **Method:** Guide E. Target: #22 NTEU official familiar with the AI/EA negotiations.
- **Probe:** *"Where's the line between a decision-support tool you'd accept and a decision-replacement tool you'd dispute? Does a durability score *in governance* cross it?"*
- ✅ **Survives if:** "evidence for academic deliberation is fine." ❌ **Killed if:** any score in governance is contested → restrict to faculty-facing, voluntary use.

**EXP-07 · Austerity-triage demand test** — Guide A · *H1/H3*
- **Levers:** P6 (cross-institutional benchmark for cut/merge/defend), P2. · **FQ4.**
- **Riskiest assumption:** in deficit, leadership will *pay* for comparative durability evidence to make portfolio decisions.
- **Method:** Guide A, lead with **portfolio-risk / cost-avoidance** framing (not "quality uplift"). Targets: #9 mid-size DVC, #10 regional DVC (where budgets are tightest — the sharpest WTP test).
- **Probe:** *"You're deciding which programs to cut, merge or defend. What evidence is in the room today — and would a comparative durability benchmark earn a budget line?"*
- ✅ **Survives if:** "this is what our portfolio review needs"; budget identified. ❌ **Killed if:** "we cut on enrolment/margin; no spend for this."

**EXP-08 · Budget-line / single-buyer test** — Guide A+C · *H3 core*
- **Levers:** P2, P6. · **FQ4.**
- **Riskiest assumption:** a recognised budget line exists for curriculum intelligence *distinct from* careers/IR's existing Lightcast spend, with a single signer.
- **Method:** Guide A probe + Guide C (data holders). Targets: #2 Dir T&L, #4 IR analyst, #9 DVC.
- **Probe:** *"Is there a budget line for curriculum intelligence separate from what careers/IR already spend on Lightcast — or does it come out of someone's hide? Who signs?"*
- ✅ **Survives if:** a named line + a single delegated signer (A$100–250k). ❌ **Killed if:** no line; a new category must be invented in every institution → H3 weak; the sale is a category-creation slog.

**EXP-09 · Regulatory-urgency test** — Guide E · *timing*
- **Lever:** P7 (productise accreditation-ready evidence on the known schedule). · **FQ7.**
- **Riskiest assumption:** the 2026 TEQSA shift + 2027–31 accreditation wave is felt as *real* anticipatory demand, not compliance theatre.
- **Method:** Guide E. Targets: #21 TEQSA-adjacent quality lead, #19 AACSB.
- **Probe:** *"What does TEQSA's 2026 shift mean practically for your team — urgency, or compliance theatre? Are you preparing evidence for the next accreditation cycle now?"*
- ✅ **Survives if:** "we need evidence frameworks now." ❌ **Killed if:** "nobody's acting early" → the timed-demand thesis (P7) softens.

**EXP-13 · Employer exposure-framing test** ✅ — *21 targets sourced; see [§ below](#-exp-13--sourced-employer-targets-gap-filled)*
- **Lever:** P10 (employer gap mis-framed as soft-skills, not exposure). · **Hyp:** H1 (framing).
- **Riskiest assumption:** employers articulate the gap as structural AI-*exposure* (COMPASS's axis) and would value a university's durability claim.
- **Method:** Mom-Test interviews with **graduate-recruitment / early-careers leads** across professional services, government, banking and tech — now sourced (warm-first, via UniMelb advisory/careers networks). Standout live case: **MinterEllison** (cut grad intake ~⅓ citing AI absorbing routine task content).
- **Probe:** *"When you say graduates aren't ready, is it polish (soft skills) or that the content itself is becoming automatable? Would a credible 'this program is AI-durable' signal change which programs you recruit from?"*
- ✅ **Survives if:** employers confirm the exposure framing and value the signal. ❌ **Killed if:** "still soft skills; a score wouldn't change our hiring" → P10 framing is wrong.

### Tier P2 — sharpen the moat

**EXP-10 · Stage-3→4 data-flow test** — Guide C · *confirms H4 is structural* · #3 Careers (Lightcast users since 2017), #4 IR, #13 NAGCAS, #14 Nous · **FQ3/6.** ✅ if "it never crosses; nobody's job" (confirms the gap is structural, not technical); ❌ if "it already flows via X" (gap less open than thought).

**EXP-11 · OSCA/JSA substrate test** — Guide D + desk · *the local moat* · #20 Jobs & Skills Australia · **FQ6.** ✅ if OSCA/JSA occupation-skills data is available on workable terms/cost/latency; ❌ if prohibitive → the free-data moat (P8) weakens, reconsider the data layer.

**EXP-12 · Accreditation-evidence acceptance** — Guide E · *deepens P4/P7* · #19 AACSB (+ Engineers Australia) · **FQ7.** ✅ if a CIR/EA panel has *accepted* quantitative labour-market alignment as evidence for a Std-4.1-type criterion; ❌ if never used/accepted → the standard-as-evidence play is premature (still viable, but on the 2027–31 horizon).

## 🛠️ Artifact tests to prepare (turns "would you want it?" into "here it is — what do you do?")

The single biggest upgrade to the interview programme. Prepare three lightweight artifacts; the *reaction* is the data.

1. **A real portfolio read** — 3–5 already-scored programs (B-DES 17/36, MC-IS 18/36, plus a couple of strong scorers) as a one-page durability dashboard. → **EXP-01, EXP-07.** Watch whether they reach for a decision.
2. **A sample redesign roadmap** — one program in the interviewee's own discipline, showing the prioritised "what to change" output. → **EXP-05.** Tests the 200-hr-burden-relief value and trust.
3. **A mock accreditation-evidence artefact** — the assessment formatted as AACSB Std 4.1 / TEQSA-facing evidence with methodology citation. → **EXP-03, EXP-09, EXP-12.** Ask: *"Would a panel accept this?"*

## 🧭 Sequencing & decision rule

**Run order (mapped to the Validate-pack waves):**

- **This week (Wave 1, warm-internal):** EXP-01, EXP-05, EXP-10, and start EXP-04 (desk) + the EXP-03 internal researchers (#7, #8). Plus **fast-track EXP-02** (warm the TechnologyOne intro now — it's time-sensitive and gates everything).
- **Next (Wave 2, 2nd-degree):** EXP-07, EXP-08, the external half of EXP-03 (#15 ACSES), and source the EXP-13 employer.
- **Then (Wave 3, cold high-value):** EXP-02 (if not already fast-tracked), EXP-06 (#22 NTEU), EXP-09 (#21), EXP-11 (#20 JSA), EXP-12 (#19 AACSB).

**The decision rule (carried from [compass-validate.md](compass-validate.md)).** After ~4–5 interviews, reassess against the convergence test: **COMPASS survives if H1 *and* H2 hold and at least one of H3/H4 holds.** Translated to this backlog:

- If **EXP-01 or EXP-03 fails** → a load-bearing assumption is broken; **loop back** (new framing/stakeholder), don't push on.
- If **EXP-02 fails** (gatekeeper closing) → **pivot-within**: partner/acquisition-target posture, fast.
- If **EXP-08 fails** but EXP-01/03 hold → demand is real but the *buyer/budget* isn't; reshape go-to-market (e.g. tie to compact/accreditation budgets), don't abandon.
- If the **P2 moat experiments** fail → adjust *how* you defend (data partnership vs build), not *whether* to proceed.

Every interview still ends with the Validate pack's three closers — *"What didn't I ask?" · "Who else?" · "Would you bet against us — why?"* — and every "who else" feeds the 22-list (especially the EXP-13 employer gap).
