# COMPASS — EXP-04: Causal-Evidence Desk Experiment

**Is the "empty category" real? A systematic search + a measurement protocol to link COMPASS durability scores to QILT graduate outcomes**

**Prepared by:** David Mulholland, Associate Director, Service Experience & Design, University of Melbourne
**Date:** 2026-06-05
**Builds on:** [Experiment Backlog](compass-experiment-backlog.md) (EXP-04 P0) · [Deeper Perspective](compass-deeper-perspective.md) ("the single biggest risk" / P12) · [Validate](compass-validate.md) (H1/H2) · [Competitive & Strategic Intelligence](compass-competitive-strategic-intelligence.md) (the "empty category" claim)

---

> **Why this matters — and a correction to the workbook.** Across the COMPASS workbook we have repeated a strong claim: *the category "independently verified, causally demonstrated outcome from LMI-informed curriculum revision" is empty.* EXP-04 set out to (a) **search whether that claim is robust** and (b) **design the measurement protocol that would fill it**. The search changed our mind on (a): **the strict, absolute claim is overstated and should be retired** — there is now credible causal evidence that updating curriculum *content* to track labour-market demand raises graduate wages. But a **sharper, narrower version of the moat survives intact**, and — importantly — the new evidence is *good* for COMPASS's premise, not bad. This document states the corrected claim, the evidence behind it, and a staged protocol to produce the first Australian, higher-education, instrument-validated evidence. (Produced by a 15-agent research workflow; confidence noted per finding.)

---

## ⚖️ Headline finding

| | Before EXP-04 | After EXP-04 |
|---|---|---|
| **The claim** | "No causal evidence links LMI-informed curriculum revision to graduate outcomes." | **Retired as stated.** Credible quasi-experimental (difference-in-differences) studies now show curriculum *content* updated to track labour-market demand **causally raises graduate wages**. |
| **What survives** | — | **A narrower, still-defensible moat:** no study causally validates a **scored durability *instrument***, against a **commercial/algorithmic LMI signal**, in **higher education** (the one clean HE-curriculum DiD is a *null*), linked to **QILT-style** outcomes, in **Australia**. |
| **Strategic read** | The empty category was framed only as a *risk*. | **It is now mostly a tailwind.** The core premise — that demand-aligned curriculum revision improves outcomes — is *evidenced*. COMPASS's open question is no longer "does any of this work?" but "does *our instrument* predict and improve outcomes *here*?" — a far better place to stand. |

**Confidence: medium** (not high). The evidentiary frontier is a November-2025 working paper, and several high-yield databases (ERIC, EconLit, Scopus, NCVER/VOCEDplus, JSA, non-English sources) were *not* systematically searched — so a closer or on-point hit cannot be excluded. This was a structured desk sweep, not a PRISMA systematic review.

## 🔍 Part A — The systematic search

### Method (and its honest limits)

Five parallel search angles: (A1) peer-reviewed HE / education-economics; (A2) adjacent proven domains; (A3) vendor & grey literature; (A4) causal-inference methodology; (A5) Australian QILT data specifics. Each distinguished **causal** designs (RCT, difference-in-differences, regression discontinuity, instrumental variables, matched-cohort with pre-trends) from correlational/descriptive/satisfaction findings. **Limits to hold in mind:** these were web/Scholar-style sweeps, not full database systematic reviews; non-English literature (the causal cluster is German/Swiss/Dutch) was read only in translation/abstract; null results are under-published (absence of evidence is partly absence of *disclosure*). Treat the conclusion as a strong working position, not a settled census.

### What the evidence looks like — four tiers

| Tier | Body of work | Design | Outcome | Matches "LMI-revised *curriculum* → outcomes"? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1 · Causal hits that break the strict claim** | VET curriculum-update DiD (Germany, Switzerland, Netherlands) | Stacked / event-study **difference-in-differences** (strong) | **Realised wages**, occupational retention | **Yes** — treatment *is* curriculum content refreshed to track evolving skill demand |
| **2 · Causal but bundled** | Sectoral-employment RCTs (WorkAdvance/Per Scholas), apprenticeship, community-college CTE | **RCT / strong QED** | Realised earnings | **Partial** — "demand-aligned focus" is one ingredient in a bundle; the curriculum-revision channel isn't isolated |
| **3 · Causal but wrong endpoint** | LMI/earnings-info → student major choice | **RCT** | Intentions / major choice, *not* realised jobs | **No** — informs students, not curriculum |
| **4 · Not causal (the confirmations)** | Graduate-employability reviews; HE curriculum-alignment frameworks; LMI-use case studies; OfS B3 | Descriptive / qualitative / threshold | Mostly self-reported skills | **No** — these are what made the category look empty |

### Tier 1 — the studies that retire the strict claim

- **Salomons, vom Baur & Zierahn-Weilage (2025), *Expertise at Work*** (working paper, Nov 2025; presented NBER/SOLE/EALE/CESifo). **Stacked difference-in-differences over 365 legally-binding German VET curriculum-update events (1971–2021)**, NLP-linked to breakthrough technologies and merged to administrative employer–employee data. **Entrants trained under updated curricula earn ~+3.3% higher wages (up to ~+5.5% in technology-exposed occupations)**; parallel trends tested; selection/composition confounds (trainee age/gender/prior schooling, exam pass rates, program selectivity, training-firm fixed effects) explicitly ruled out. The curriculum is updated through an institutionalised employer–union–BIBB labour-market-feedback process — *structurally an LMI→curriculum loop*.
- **Buehler/Lehnert & Backes-Gellner (2025), *Journal of Education and Work* 37(5–6)** — independent replication on the **universe of Swiss VET updates** (NLP "novelty/removal" rates), corroborated by a Netherlands (mbo) working paper.

### Tier 2 — the most valuable *transferable* cite

- **Katz, Roth, Hendra & Schaberg (2020/2022), "Why Do Sectoral Employment Programs Work?" (WorkAdvance, NBER w28248; RCT).** +11–40% earnings; Per Scholas sustained ~+20% at 6–10 years. **The decisive nugget:** the earnings payoff comes specifically from *steering content/placement toward higher-wage occupations and industries* — **labour-market alignment is the active ingredient**, over and above screening and wraparound services. COMPASS's theory of change is therefore **mechanism-consistent with RCT evidence**, while being candid that the *intervention form* (degree-curriculum revision) is untested.
- Supporting: **Stevens, Kurlaender & Grosz (2019, *J. Human Resources*)** — community-college CTE returns 14–45%, largest in occupation-specific fields, on linked admin+earnings data (the design template COMPASS needs); **Reed et al. (2012)** apprenticeship QED (+7.8–8.8pp employment).

### Why even the strongest hit "falls short" for COMPASS (the surviving moat)

Salomons et al. is genuinely causal but **off-category on every axis COMPASS depends on**: it is **vocational, not higher-ed**; the demand signal is patent/technology exposure via *legally-binding* employer–union negotiation, **not a commercial LMI product or a scored durability rubric**; the authors concede the *coordinated, binding* curriculum is itself part of the causal mechanism (a serious **external-validity threat** to autonomous, self-accrediting Australian universities); and the outcome is individual worker wages, **not a program-level QILT metric in Australia**. The one clean *higher-education* curriculum-DiD located is a **null**.

### The reframed, falsifiable claim (use this across the workbook)

> ~~"No causal evidence links LMI-informed curriculum revision to graduate outcomes."~~
> **"Demand-aligned curriculum revision is now *causally evidenced* to raise wages — but in legally-binding VET systems, via structural (not instrument-based) signals, measured on worker wages, not in Australian higher education. No study yet validates that a *scored durability instrument* predicts or improves *QILT* graduate outcomes for *Australian university programs* — which is the evidence COMPASS can be first to produce."**

This is both more honest and more useful: it concedes the mechanism works (strengthening the "why now"), and names the precise, ownable gap.

## 🧪 Part B — The measurement protocol (COMPASS score → QILT outcomes)

Three candidate designs were generated and scored by a methodologist judge. The Tier-1 VET DiD studies are the **methodological template**.

| Design | Internal validity | AU feasibility | Speed to signal | Accreditor credibility | Role |
|---|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|---|
| **1 · Staggered-adoption DiD** (Callaway–Sant'Anna; treated = COMPASS-redesigned programs vs not-yet-redesigned, within-institution unit records) | **5** | 3 | 1 (5–7 yrs) | **5** | **Robustness / confirmatory arm** |
| **2 · Cross-sectional predictive-validity** (does a frozen baseline score forecast subsequent QILT outcomes, with rich controls) | 2 | **5** | **5** (<1 yr) | 3 | The fast leading indicator |
| **3 · PROSPECT-DiD** (pre-registered prospective cohort: Layer 1 = predictive back-test; Layer 2 = embedded staggered DiD on redesigns already in flight) | 4 | 4 | 4 | **5** | **★ Recommended PRIMARY (umbrella)** |

### The recommended programme: one staged study under a single pre-registration

The judge's key insight: these are **not three rival studies** — they are one staged programme, and the **pre-registration with cryptographically frozen baseline scores is the connective tissue and the moat artefact**. Run **PROSPECT-DiD (Design 3)** as the umbrella; the standalone DiD (Design 1) *is* its Layer 2 run to maturity.

- **Step 0 — months 0–3 (~free): pre-register and freeze.** Lodge a public pre-registration (OSF/AEA) declaring the frozen 10+1-dimension rubric (the schema already exists in [compass-static/src/data/programData.ts](compass-static/src/data/programData.ts) — 10 dimensions + Irreplaceability bonus, 0–3 each, /36, RESILIENT→CRITICAL bands), the matching protocol (ASCED 6-digit field-of-education + level + intake composition), the estimand (ATT via Callaway–Sant'Anna), the primary outcome (GOS full-time-employment %), the n<25 pooling rule, and the inference plan. **Cryptographically freeze + timestamp** baseline scores for ~15–30 University of Melbourne programs and record dated redesign-effective indicators for the 2–6 already slated for revision. *This freeze defeats score→outcome leakage — the single most important credibility safeguard — and the pre-registration is itself an artefact accreditors and faculty reward, available before any outcome lands.*
- **Step 1 — months 3–9 (the cheapest credible first signal): back-test predictive validity.** Under one HREC secondary-use approval, join retrospectively-frozen scores to the partner's **own already-collected GOS/GOS-L unit-record files** at 6-digit ASCED, pool 3–5 cohorts to beat suppression, and report calibration + discrimination (AUC) + incremental ΔR²/ΔAUC over a confounder-only baseline, with E-value/negative-control sensitivity. **First publishable result in <1 year** — positive = the score validates; near-zero = a publishable confirmation of the (narrow) empty category. *Either way it is the highest-value research outcome.*
- **Step 2 — months 9–18:** extend the back-test to already-collected GOS-L waves for a same-window *medium-term* predictive read, and formalise the embedded DiD treated/control arms.
- **Step 3 — years 2–7:** let the staggered-adoption DiD mature as fully-revised cohorts reach GOS then GOS-L, reporting interim event-study pre-trend leads as they accrue. Only after Step 1 shows signal worth scaling do you add the heavy upgrades — multi-institution consortium HRECs, stepped-wedge/waitlist randomisation of *rollout order* (the design-based upgrade that converts conditional parallel trends to as-good-as-random), and **PLIDA/BLADE ATO-linked verified earnings** via ABS DataLab to replace self-reported salary.

### The QILT realities that constrain the design (and the mitigations)

- **Granularity mismatch.** COMPASS scores a *named program*; QILT's smallest *published* unit is institution × 21-study-area (coarser). **The only way to align a score to a specific program/major is the institution's own GOS/GOS-L unit-record file** (6-digit ASCED course-FOE, + major where the institution opted to survey at major level). This is **already lawful within existing QILT governance** — no new data-sharing deal needed.
- **Small cells.** The n<25 suppression rule routinely blanks program cells; only 40.3% of strata met QILT's own ±7.5pp precision target in 2024; GOS-L is thinner still. **Mitigation: pool across multiple cohorts; treat single-cell estimates as noisy; favour change-over-time and pooled designs.**
- **Latency.** A 2026-effective revision → first fully-revised completers ~end-2027 → first GOS read in the 2028 cycle; GOS-L (the decision-relevant earnings/skills-utilisation signal) ~2031–32. **This lag is *why* the staged design front-loads the back-test.**
- **The ANZSCO→OSCA seam (a live hazard).** Occupation coding is mid-migration (ABS OSCA from Dec 2024; Census/stats ~Sep 2026); it is **not a 1:1 remap** (splits/merges; skill-level recalibration). **Mitigation: anchor scores at the stable ASCED field-of-education level, *not* occupation; avoid occupation-skill-level metrics across the seam; version-pin every LMI input.** *Failing this, a reclassification artefact could masquerade as a durability change.*
- **Confounding is severe** (QILT's own caveat: outcomes are driven by field, intake composition, study mode, and regional labour markets, not just teaching) — **which is exactly why a credible protocol must lean on staggered-timing DiD, not cross-sectional correlation**, and run a placebo-on-inputs check (ATAR/SEIFA as a separate outcome).
- **Ethics & data governance.** The validation *join* happens at the individual record → **HREC secondary-use review is required** (graduates consented to a survey, not to validating a commercial product); adopt QILT's own n<25 discipline; **run all joins inside the partner university's environment — unit records never exported to COMPASS.**

### Minimum viable version

A pre-revenue venture can run **Step 0 + Step 1 alone** for near-zero cost on data the University of Melbourne already holds, producing a defensible predictive-validity result (or a clean null) within a year — and the pre-registration artefact immediately. Everything heavier is deferred until that signal justifies it.

## 🎯 Implications for the venture

1. **Reframe the "biggest risk."** [compass-deeper-perspective.md](compass-deeper-perspective.md) called the empty category COMPASS's "largest credibility risk and most defensible moat." Half of that is now *better*: the mechanism is evidenced, so the **risk shrinks** and the **"why now" strengthens**. The moat narrows to instrument-validation-in-Australian-HE — still ownable, and now with a concrete protocol to claim it.
2. **A new external-validity question to carry into interviews.** The Tier-1 evidence depends on *legally-binding, nationally-coordinated* curricula, which the authors treat as part of the mechanism. Australia's self-accrediting, autonomous universities are the opposite. **Does the wage payoff survive without coordination?** This is a sharp question for the EXP-03 standing interviews (CSHE, LH Martin) and reinforces why COMPASS's *standard-setting* play (manufacturing a soft coordination point) matters.
3. **This protocol *is* the H2 (standing) answer.** A pre-registered, methodologically-credible measurement programme — even before results land — is precisely the artefact that lets an accreditor or faculty body treat the durability signal as evidence rather than vendor opinion. **EXP-04 and EXP-03 are coupled.**
4. **Cheapest next move:** lodge the Step-0 pre-registration and run the Step-1 back-test on UoM's own GOS files under HREC — a sub-12-month, near-zero-cost path to the first credible "predicted→validated" signal.
