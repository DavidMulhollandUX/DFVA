Context

The core challenge

Programs are typically designed against a labour market of three to five years ago. Generative AI has widened that lag, with consequences for graduate outcomes.

By 2030 the World Economic Forum projects churn equal to 22% of today’s jobs — about 262 million — with 39% of workers’ core skills disrupted1; yet only 24% of Australian workers report AI training, against 64% in India and China.2

01 The missing metric

No standard way to assess whether a program is likely to retain its labour-market value five years out.

02 Slow review cycles

Without systematic labour-market data, curriculum review tends to be slow, anecdotal, and resource-intensive.

03 The employer cost

Employers absorb the cost of graduates who enter the workforce underprepared for AI-augmented practice.

The Framework

The eleven dimensions of durability

The AQF, TEQSA’s Higher Education Standards, and professional accreditation all measure curriculum quality and compliance — none measure whether graduate roles survive AI automation. Evidura scores exactly that: the durability of graduate work against AI task-displacement from 2027 onward.

Each program is scored 0–3 across ten core dimensions plus an irreplaceability bonus; the renormalised total (out of 36) determines the risk band.

D1 · Automation exposure

Do early-career roles cluster in automatable tasks, or in judgement, design, and accountability?

D2 · Systems thinking

Is hypothesis formation, constraint reasoning, and failure-mode analysis explicitly assessed?

D3 · Technical & quantitative depth

Is there substantive depth in statistics, data, computation, or domain science?

D4 · Decision-making under uncertainty

Does assessment require defended trade-offs under real constraints rather than recall?

D5 · AI literacy & governance

Can graduates design, supervise, and critique AI systems and their governance?

D6 · Domain depth & specialisation

Does the degree build durable specialist expertise that takes years to acquire?

D7 · Research methods rigour

Do graduates generate primary evidence and defend methodology under scrutiny?

D8 · Human & relational capability

Are interpersonal, ethical, or clinical capabilities assessed as core competencies?

D9 · Curriculum currency

Does the program use feedback loops and outcome tracking to remain current?

D10 · Graduate outcome evidence

Does the institution publish granular post-graduation employment evidence?

B · Irreplaceability premium · bonus

Does it fuse technical depth, domain expertise, and human judgement to resist substitution?
