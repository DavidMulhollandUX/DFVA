Destiny Solutions (Toronto) built the continuing education SIS that became Destiny One and later Lifelong Learning. The two companies merged in 2019, forming the entity that would be rebranded Modern Campus in September 2021.

Between 2021 and 2022 the company executed six bolt-on acquisitions in rapid succession:

## Product

### Core Modules

**1. Modern Campus Curriculum (formerly Curriculog)**
The primary product in the COMPASS competitive set. A curriculum lifecycle management platform built around multi-step approval workflow automation.

Core capabilities:
- Configurable multi-step approval workflow with parallel and sequential routing
- Proposal origination for courses and programs by faculty and staff
- Real-time commenting and revision tracking with full audit trail
- Centralized curriculum repository propagating changes to Catalog (Acalog) and connected SIS systems
- Role-based access for originators, reviewers, and administrators
- Downstream integration with Modern Campus Catalog for publishing
- Reporting and documentation for accreditation purposes (SACSCOC Principle 2.1, HLC Criterion 1)

Vendor claim: up to 50% reduction in approval cycle times. *(Confidence: High on feature set; Medium on the cycle-time claim as independently unverifiable)*

**2. Modern Campus Catalog (formerly Acalog)**
Course catalog management system. Approximately 800 college customers. Provides the downstream publishing destination for curriculum proposals approved through the Curriculum product. The closed-loop Curriculum → Catalog workflow is Modern Campus's primary integration differentiator. *(Confidence: High)*

**3. Modern Campus Omni CMS (formerly OmniUpdate OU Campus)**
Higher-education web content management system. 550+ institutions. Not directly relevant to COMPASS competitive positioning but represents the largest single product in the portfolio by customer count. *(Confidence: High)*

**4. Modern Campus Lifelong Learning (formerly Destiny One + Lumens)**
SIS and enrollment management for non-traditional and continuing education. 380+ institutions. Includes out-of-box Ellucian Colleague support and a dedicated Workday Marketplace app ('Modern Connect'). *(Confidence: High)*

**5. Modern Campus Involve (formerly Presence)**
Student engagement and co-curricular tracking. Connects engagement activity data to retention dashboards. The outcomes chain is: engagement data → dashboard → leadership justification. No predictive capability confirmed. *(Confidence: High)*

**6. Modern Campus Message (formerly Signal Vine)**
SMS and text messaging campaigns. Includes 'AI-enhanced' messaging framing (see AI features section). *(Confidence: High)*

**7. Modern Campus Schedule**
Class scheduling module within the Connected Curriculum suite. Registrars have described the scheduling capability as sub-par relative to dedicated scheduling tools such as Coursedog. *(Confidence: Medium — sourced from practitioner commentary)*

**8. Modern Campus Career Pathways**
Content recommendation tool that surfaces career pathway suggestions to students browsing institutional websites. This is a front-end personalisation feature, not an institutional analytics tool. It cannot predict graduate employment outcomes or assess programme viability. *(Confidence: High)*

### Implementation Timeline

CMS product implementation: approximately 3–4 months from discovery to go-live. The documented breakdown:
- 1–2 months: partner IT/marketing asset provision
- 2–4 weeks: branding/SSO/data validation
- 1 month: integration
- 2–4 weeks: initial administrator training
- 2 weeks: additional training
- Ongoing: monthly support

Curriculum (formerly Curriculog) implementation timelines are not publicly disclosed. Given the multi-step approval workflow configuration requirements (Level I, II, III approvals; SIS data imports; form launch sequences; role mapping), Curriculum onboarding is assessed as at least as complex as CMS onboarding. Secondary evidence supports this: ECU (East Carolina University) published a detailed FAQ describing multi-level approval routing and data import sequences as requiring careful technical knowledge; CSU Ohio published a formal Technical Review Checklist for the product. *(Confidence: Medium — CMS timeline confirmed; Curriculum timeline estimated)*

## Messaging and Positioning

### Core Value Proposition

Primary tagline: **"Attract. Engage. Retain. Learners For Life."**

Overarching framework: **The Learner-to-Earner Lifecycle™** — a lifecycle platform designed to manage every student touchpoint from prospect to alumni. The pitch is a unified platform replacing fragmented point solutions across web, catalog, curriculum, engagement, messaging, and CE enrollment.

The core efficiency claim is workflow automation and personalisation at scale. Modern Campus does not lead with analytical intelligence; it leads with operational efficiency, experience design, and platform connectedness. *(Confidence: High)*

### Platform Strategy

Modern Campus positions as '100% higher-education-exclusive SaaS' competing against generic CRMs and fragmented point tools. The transformation narrative is externally driven — institutions face 'radical transformation' from declining enrolments and rising costs, and Modern Campus helps them 'keep pace.'

The cross-sell logic operates as: CMS customer → personalisation engine → Lifelong Learning (CE/workforce SIS) → Involve (engagement) → Curriculum/Catalog. The Modern Campus Advantage program (launched March 2023) reduces friction for existing customers adopting additional suite products. Growth strategy is lifecycle bundling from a 1,700+ institution installed base, not analytics upsell. *(Confidence: High)*

### AI Narrative

Modern Campus's AI narrative is thin and narrowly applied. Their only AI-labelled product is the Message platform's AI-enhanced SMS personalisation — automated chatbot flows and engagement nudges. The curriculum product page describes value exclusively through 'automated workflows' and 'up to 50% reduction in approval time.' No AI-driven curriculum design, no predictive capability, no labour market signal integration is claimed for the curriculum suite.

The broader positioning uses 'automation' and 'digital transformation' language in contexts where 'AI' would be the contemporary framing — suggesting the marketing team has noted the gap but the product has not closed it. *(Confidence: High)*

### Competitive Framing

Modern Campus's competitive framing positions against:
- Generic CRMs (Salesforce-in-HE): "we're built specifically for higher ed"
- Point solutions/fragmented tools: "we connect the lifecycle vs. managing tools in silos"
- Legacy homegrown systems: "workflow modernisation and compliance automation"

Modern Campus does not explicitly position against COMPASS-category tools (programme viability analytics, labour market intelligence) because that category is not yet established in their competitive frame. This is an opportunity: COMPASS enters a market space Modern Campus is not defending. *(Confidence: High)*

### Content Themes and Thought Leadership

Modern Campus produces operational content: blog posts, webinars (branded 'web events'), product roadmap sessions, CMS update notes, student engagement challenge guides, text messaging strategy guides. Content targets practitioners — registrars, web teams, student affairs staff.

There is no evidence of thought leadership on curriculum strategy, programme portfolio management, future-of-work alignment, or market intelligence for academic planning. Modern Campus is not in the conversation that COMPASS is positioning to lead. *(Confidence: Medium)*

## COMPASS Positioning Against Modern Campus

### One-Sentence Differentiation

Modern Campus tells institutions whether their curriculum was approved; COMPASS tells them whether it should exist.

### When COMPASS Wins

- An Australian university is asking whether its programme portfolio is viable in a changing labour market and no existing vendor answers that question
- A program director or dean needs evidence to support a new programme proposal or kill a failing one, and the accreditation-compliance workflow of Modern Campus does not provide that evidence
- A QA lead needs to demonstrate that programmes meet AI-era graduate capability standards — a question Modern Campus cannot operationalise
- A provost is facing government pressure on graduate employment outcomes and needs programme-level analytics, not approval records
- A university is frustrated with the implementation complexity and operational overhead of Modern Campus's multi-product suite and wants a focused intelligence tool
- Any institution in Australia or APAC where Modern Campus has no presence, no local support, no TEQSA alignment, and no established commercial relationship

### When COMPASS Loses

- An institution has already invested heavily in Modern Campus Curriculum and the switching cost of changing approval workflow infrastructure is prohibitive — COMPASS should position as a complement, not a replacement, in these accounts
- The buyer is primarily IT-led and the decision criterion is SIS integration coverage (Banner, Colleague, Workday native integrations) — Modern Campus wins on infrastructure depth
- A US or Canadian institution is consolidating vendors and seeking a single-platform deal from an established 1,700-customer provider — Modern Campus's platform breadth wins this conversation
- The buying trigger is accreditation cycle documentation and the institution needs SACSCOC or HLC audit trail capability — Modern Campus has 5+ years of established positioning here
- Budget holder is the registrar, not the dean or provost — Modern Campus owns the registrar relationship; COMPASS does not yet have practitioner penetration

### Objection Handling

**"We already have Curriculog / Modern Campus Curriculum."**
Modern Campus Curriculum manages the approval of curriculum decisions. COMPASS assesses whether those decisions are strategically sound — whether the programme will produce employable graduates, whether it reflects AI-era capability requirements, whether it is viable in the current market. These are different questions. COMPASS integrates with or runs alongside workflow tools; it is not a replacement for approval infrastructure.

**"Modern Campus is used at 850+ campuses — they must be doing something right."**
Scale of adoption confirms that Modern Campus solves the workflow problem well. It does not confirm they solve the intelligence problem, because they do not attempt to. Market share in a workflow category is not evidence of capability in a viability intelligence category.

**"Modern Campus has PE backing and will add AI features soon."**
Plausible. Providence Equity's investment thesis explicitly includes further acquisitions. However, adding AI features to a workflow tool does not make it a strategic intelligence tool — these require different data assets (labour market data, graduate outcomes data, sector benchmarks), different analytical models, and different buyer relationships. COMPASS should establish reference customers and thought leadership before Modern Campus can acquire its way into this space.

**"We don't have budget for another SaaS tool."**
COMPASS is not a workflow replacement — it does not require ripping out existing systems. The comparison is not 'COMPASS vs Curriculog'; it is 'COMPASS vs making programme portfolio decisions without evidence.' The alternative to COMPASS is not Modern Campus; it is uninformed curriculum strategy.

**"We're a US institution — why would we look at an Australian product?"**
COMPASS is built on a University of Melbourne research base and is designed for export. The analytical framework (AI-era dimensional scoring) is universally applicable. The Australian regulatory context (TEQSA, AQF) makes certain modules Australia-specific, but the core viability scoring is not geography-bound.

### Weaknesses COMPASS Directly Addresses in Modern Campus Accounts

1. **Zero AI or intelligence in curriculum decisions** — COMPASS provides the analytical layer Modern Campus is missing
2. **No graduate employment outcome capability** — COMPASS connects programme decisions to employment evidence
3. **No labour market signal integration** — COMPASS ingests real-time market demand to score programme relevance
4. **Australia/APAC market absence** — COMPASS has no incumbent to displace; every Australian university is a greenfield prospect
5. **High buyer altitude mismatch** — Modern Campus is designed for registrars and web teams; COMPASS speaks the language of deans, provosts, and QA leads
6. **Platform complexity fatigue** — COMPASS is a focused intelligence tool, not a tenth product in an acquisition-assembled suite
