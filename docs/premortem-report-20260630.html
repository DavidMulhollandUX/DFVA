Evidura Is Dead.
Here's How It Died.

A prospective hindsight analysis of the Evidura business model —
9 failure scenarios investigated by 9 independent agents
June 30, 2026 · 6-month horizon · December 2026 failure date

Most Likely Failure

Independence + procurement velocity compound fatally. Competitor universities see a UoM-incubated rating tool as a protection racket, not an independent service — and even the ones that would buy can't, because university procurement takes 12–18 months for a new category with no budget line item. The Go8 design partners who participated free in Phase 1 balk at the $49K invoice. Sales velocity: 3–4 institutions/year, not 12. Year 3 ARR: ~$300K, not $1.6M. Break-even never arrives. The spin-out burns its seed funding and closes quietly.

Most Dangerous Failure

The LLM methodology is successfully challenged, and the entire venture's academic credibility collapses. A CRITICAL-rated program (Screenwriting, 11/36) disputes the score. An external review finds: no inter-rater reliability testing, LLM hallucinations in scoring rationales, handbook content variability making scores incomparable, and zero human verification of any pilot rating. One news cycle. One credible external review. The methodology is dead — and with it, every revenue line, every partnership conversation, and the entire brand proposition. This kills the product itself, not just the business model.

The Hidden Assumption

Universities want to be publicly rated. Every element of the plan — the certification seal, the public index, the "show me the Evidura rating" Carfax moment, the demand-pull pricing model — assumes that universities will pay to have their programs scored, banded, and compared. But universities are reputation-protection machines operating in a prestige economy. A CRITICAL rating is not a sales opportunity — it's an institutional liability they will suppress, legally challenge, and lobby against. The Moody's analogy breaks at the most fundamental level: bond issuers need a rating to access capital markets. Universities already have self-accreditation authority and a government-regulated monopoly on degree-granting. They don't need Evidura. They need Evidura not to exist.

The Revised Plan

Seven concrete changes to make the model survivable:

1. Pivot from public rating agency → private benchmarking service. Ratings are confidential to the purchasing institution. No public seal, no public index, no published comparisons. The McKinsey model: they pay for the analysis, they own the output. This kills the demand-resistance problem and the legal challenge risk simultaneously.

2. Resolve independence before any competitor university is asked to pay. Founder resigns from University of Melbourne employment, or the venture is transferred to an independent entity with no UoM equity. This cannot be deferred to Year 2 — every Phase 1 design-partner relationship is poisoned by the dual role.

3. Kill the accreditation-body revenue line. AACSB and AMC are distribution channels, not customers. They do not buy third-party tools. Remove $240K–$360K from Year 3 projections. Replace with: universities pay because accreditation bodies signal demand, not because the bodies themselves pay.

4. Re-baseline sales velocity to 3–5 institutions/year. Year 3 target drops from 26 to 8–12. Break-even shifts from Month 20 to Month 42. Capital requirement rises from $500K–$800K to $1.2M–$1.8M. Accept the timeline or raise accordingly.

5. Commission multi-rater reliability study before any external sale. 3 human curriculum design experts + 2 alternative LLMs score the same 10 programs. Publish the inter-rater reliability results (target >0.8 Cohen's kappa). Until this is done, "LLM-assisted, human-verified" is a plan, not a practice — and an external challenge will expose it.

6. Accept the $1.5–$2M ARR ceiling for Australia. The $10M Year 5 ambition requires 400 international institutions and $23M–$27M in growth capital that the unit economics cannot generate. Either raise venture funding at that scale, or accept the lifestyle-business ceiling. The "lifestyle business" framing in the plan is not a failure — it's a $1.5M ARR company with 60%+ gross margins. That's a real business.

7. Drop agent-native distribution as a revenue channel. Reclassify MCP tools as developer infrastructure only. Zero institutional buyers will be reached through AI agent citations in Years 1–3. The bandwidth spent on this channel should be redirected to procurement-relationship building.

The 9 Ways It Died — Full Agent Investigations

Critical

1. Independence Perceived as Sham

Go8 design partners participate free, see UoM programs scoring highest on a UoM-built methodology, then receive a $49K invoice from a founder still employed by UoM. "Melbourne built a tool to grade the rest of us, gave themselves the highest scores, and now wants us to pay." The spin-out can't retroactively cure the origin-story capture. 38 of 41 universities view Evidura as a UoM commercialisation play.
Underlying Assumption
Structural separation (spin-out + board + governance charter) can retroactively cure the perception of captured independence created by the incubation period.
Warning Sign
Founder's dual role (UoM Associate Director + Evidura CEO) is visible from Day 0. A rating agency whose chief rating officer draws a salary from one of the institutions being rated cannot credibly claim impartiality.
Critical

2. Universities Suppress Ratings, Don't Buy Them

The pilot found 0% RESILIENT, 83% MODERATE, 15% HIGH RISK, 2% CRITICAL. The tool is structurally negative — it tells most programs they're at risk. The first CRITICAL rating generates a legal threat, not a sales inquiry. Universities don't operate in a market where negative public ratings drive improvement — they operate in a prestige economy where negative ratings are suppressed through legal, political, and peak-body channels.
Underlying Assumption
Demand pull from rating subjects — that universities will want to be publicly rated and benchmarked. The Moody's/S&P analogy breaks at the most fundamental level: universities don't need a rating to access anything.
Warning Sign
If the first questions from design partners are "can we opt out?" and "do we get right of reply?" rather than "how do we buy?" — you're being threat-assessed, not vendor-evaluated.
High

3. Accreditation Bodies Never Pay

AACSB is a membership organisation, not a procurement entity. Individual staff cannot endorse a vendor's instrument. Standards revision cycles run 3–5 years from conversation to mandate. There is no precedent for an accreditation body paying a startup to embed its methodology. The $240K–$360K/year "per body" revenue line is a category error — accreditation bodies are distribution channels (they create demand among universities), not customers (they do not buy).
Underlying Assumption
Accreditation bodies behave like enterprise buyers — that they can evaluate, purchase, and deploy a tool within 12–24 months.
Warning Sign
Falsifiable Question 7 is unanswered: "Has quantitative labour-market data been used as evidence in AACSB CIR peer reviews?" If no answer exists from secondary research, the revenue line is built on hope, not evidence.
High

4. Benchmark Dataset Never Reaches Critical Mass

26 Australian institutions = 26 data points. That's not a benchmark — most discipline × institution-type cells have N=1 or N=2. International buyers need comparisons against their own sector, not Australia's. But the plan's action item #7 schedules international dataset seeding after revenue targets, creating a circular dependency: can't sell internationally without benchmark data, can't build benchmark data without international customers. Lightcast enters in Year 4 with an internationally-rich dataset built from 18B+ labour market data points — no institutional relationships required.
Underlying Assumption
The Australian benchmark dataset will be valuable enough to sell internationally — and international customers will buy it before the international dataset exists to make it valuable.
Warning Sign
The business model itself acknowledges the Australian TAM is only $1.0M–$1.5M in institutional subscriptions. If the dataset isn't valuable at 26 points, the international pathway doesn't exist.
Critical

5. Unit Economics Don't Close

Year 3 at $218K EBITDA cannot fund international expansion. Acquiring 360 international institutions requires $6.48M–$18M at realistic CAC — against $218K retained earnings. The plan's own "One Chart That Matters" shows a mathematically impossible 9.6× EBITDA jump from Year 3 to Year 4 ($218K → $2.1M) with no capital bridge. The business plateaus at $1.2M–$1.6M ARR — a failure only by its own $10M ambition. Realistic capital requirement: $23.5M–$26.9M, not $500K–$800K.
Underlying Assumption
The business can self-fund international expansion from Australian operating profits. At realistic CAC, the shortfall is $12M–$18M.
Warning Sign
The 400-institution Year 5 target exceeds the ~318 universities in the named markets (AU/NZ/UK/CA). Even 100% market penetration doesn't hit the number.
High

6. Well-Funded Competitor Enters & Gives Product Away Free

Lightcast (KKR-backed, 5 acquisitions in 2025) already has Australian distribution through Labour Insight and an existing customer base. In 2027, they announce Skillabi for Australia — free for the first year. Every Evidura procurement conversation freezes. Year 2 revenue drops from $920K projected to $87K actual. The 18–36 month "window" was never a window — it was a grace period from a competitor who hadn't yet decided to care. KKR can make the $1.5M Australian market uneconomic with a rounding-error budget.
Underlying Assumption
The 18–36 month first-mover window is guaranteed. It was a discovery assumption (they haven't noticed) that collapsed into a caring assumption (they haven't bothered yet).
Warning Sign
Lightcast's 5 acquisitions in 2025 signal active roll-up mode. Australian university margins at 5.3% mean no budget for premium pricing when a free alternative exists.
Critical

7. LLM Scoring Methodology Successfully Challenged

March 2027: the State of Degree Durability index launches, featuring MC-SCWR Screenwriting at 11/36 CRITICAL. The program director publishes an open letter. A commissioned external review finds: no inter-rater reliability testing, LLM hallucinations in scoring rationales, handbook content variability making scores incomparable, and zero human verification of any pilot rating. One news cycle. The methodology is dead. AACSB withdraws from all conversations. Go8 partners demand contract termination. Revenue → $0 within 90 days.
Underlying Assumption
An LLM, given a sufficiently structured prompt, can produce scoring outputs reliable enough for external publication without human verification.
Warning Sign
The methodology document §5 explicitly states no inter-rater reliability testing has been conducted. This is not a hidden vulnerability — it's a publicly documented one.
Critical

8. Agent-Native Channel Generates Zero Demand

The MCP tools are elegant. Six clean endpoints. AI agents can query Evidura ratings programmatically. But the people who use AI coding agents (developers) are not the buyers (DVC-Academics, Deans). No institutional purchasing decision in history has been driven by an AI agent citation. The channel is structurally decoupled from revenue — removing it from the financial model changes zero projections. Bandwidth spent on MCP tooling in Years 1–3 is bandwidth not spent on procurement relationships.
Underlying Assumption
AI agent citations will translate into institutional purchasing decisions — a causal chain with zero observed instances.
Warning Sign
No agent-native revenue line item exists in the P&L. The channel is described as "seeding demand" but has no measurable output, no attributable revenue, and no accountability timeline.
High

9. University Procurement Kills Sales Velocity

The plan assumes 26 institutions by Year 3 — 63% penetration of Australian universities. But university procurement for a new category with no budget line item takes 12–18 months, during active cost-cutting (margins at 5.3%). Even enthusiastic Deans can't buy what has no budget code. Actual velocity: 3–4 institutions per year. Year 3: 5–8 institutions, not 26. Break-even pushes from Month 20 to Month 42. The "relationship-driven" sales approach is inherently slow — each DVC conversation takes months.
Underlying Assumption
University procurement behaves like enterprise B2B SaaS — 9-month CAC payback, 8–9 new signings per year. The $18K CAC and 9-month payback are SaaS benchmarks, not university procurement benchmarks.
Warning Sign
The risk table classifies procurement velocity as MEDIUM severity. The corrected model shows it's the binding constraint on the entire financial plan — it should be CRITICAL.

Pre-Launch Survival Checklist

1
Multi-rater reliability study published. 3 human curriculum design experts + 2 alternative LLMs score the same 10 programs. Inter-rater reliability target >0.8 Cohen's kappa. Until this is done, do not sell the methodology to anyone.
2
Independence structure resolved before first external invoice. Founder resigns from UoM employment, or the venture is transferred to an independent entity with no UoM equity, no UoM board seat, and a published governance charter. This is not deferrable to Year 2 — every Phase 1 conversation is poisoned without it.
3
First 3 paying customers secured through existing relationships before any cold procurement outreach. If you can't sell to people who already trust you, you can't sell to anyone.
4
Legal review of methodology completed. Errors-and-omissions insurance in place. Formal legal opinion on the classification of DFVA ratings as opinion (like a credit rating), not fact. Defamation risk assessed and mitigated.
5
"No public ratings without institutional consent" policy codified. The Carfax/Health Star Rating public-demand model is killed. Ratings are confidential to the purchasing institution. If the certification seal is ever launched, it's opt-in only, RESILIENT-band only.
6
Procurement pathway validated. Interview 3 DVCs-Academic at non-UoM institutions. Ask: "If you wanted to buy this, what budget line would it come from and how long would it take?" Do not build the sales pipeline until you know the answer.
7
Competitor monitoring active. Set Google Alerts / news monitoring for Lightcast, Mapademics, Coursedog, and "curriculum AI" in Australian higher education. The 18-36 month window is an assumption — verify it monthly.
