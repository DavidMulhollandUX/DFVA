### 1.7 Professional Accreditation Bodies — Economics and AI Stance
**Status:** [x] COMPLETED — June 2026 | Full findings in [compass-competitive-strategic-intelligence.md](compass-competitive-strategic-intelligence.md)

**Key findings:**
- **Only the AMC (medicine) has an explicit AI/digital health standard** — 2024 accreditation standards + Digital Capability Framework for Medicine (2021, AI literacy for practitioners)
- **AACSB (business) is the strongest commercial opening** — Standard 4.1 requires "agility with current and emerging technologies"; 97% of schools mention AI; AI Use Case Hub launched; 15 AU schools; CIR 5→6yr from July 2026
- Engineers Australia, APAC, and law (Priestley 11, unchanged since 1992) have **no named AI criterion**
- **Structural lag insight:** 4–6yr accreditation cycles vs. AI change means AI requirements won't be compulsory until 2027–2031 → anticipatory evidence demand now
- A durability tool aligns most directly with AACSB Standard 4.1 and the AMC digital health standard — positioned as generating *accreditation-relevant evidence*

### 1.8 International Case Studies — Successful LMI-to-Curriculum Integration
**Status:** [x] COMPLETED — June 2026 | Full findings in [compass-competitive-strategic-intelligence.md](compass-competitive-strategic-intelligence.md)

**Key findings:**
- **The "independently verified causal outcome" category is EMPTY** — no peer-reviewed study proves LMI-informed curriculum revision improves graduate employment outcomes
- **St. Catherine 44% claim is vendor-published only** — university quote doesn't cite the figure; describes Skillabi use as "early." Treat with caution
- **Verified process examples:** University of Strathclyde (LMI in careers/course review, MEDIUM); Dallas College LMIC (best community-college governance, MEDIUM-HIGH process/LOW outcomes); **Texas HB 8** (outcomes-based funding mandate — strongest, MEDIUM-HIGH)
- **Key lesson:** funding incentives activate LMI-curriculum alignment more effectively than voluntary tool adoption
- **Australian cautionary tale — Job-ready Graduates (2020):** 113% humanities fee rise shifted enrolments only 3pp. LMI signals applied bluntly to steer behaviour fail when student interest/capability/aspiration are the real drivers
- Success conditions: dedicated staffing, formal governance, multi-source integration, employer validation, embedded in review cycles. Central barrier: "the presence of labour market data does not guarantee that colleges will use it"

## Priority Falsifiable Questions for Primary Research

These eight questions emerged from the completed secondary research (rounds 1–2). Each can only be answered by talking to people inside the system, and each is specific and falsifiable. They are the spine of the interview program — every conversation should be designed to confirm or refute one or more.

1. **Curriculum change authority (AACSB schools):** Who holds formal sign-off for changes to program learning outcomes, and what documentary evidence initiates a review cycle?
2. **TechnologyOne/CourseLoop LMI roadmap:** Has TechnologyOne internally scoped or contracted any LMI integration into CourseLoop's 2026A/2026B roadmap? If not, what is the stated position on LMI as a feature? *(The unoccupied adjacency — the single most consequential market variable.)*
3. **Careers teams' access to curriculum governance:** At universities with a DVC (Education, Experience and Employability), do careers teams hold standing representation on curriculum committees, or transmit LMI informally on request?
4. **Willingness to pay:** Among DVCs (Education) at mid-tier (non-Go8) universities, is there a recognised budget line or procurement appetite for curriculum intelligence software distinct from careers teams' existing LMI subscriptions?
5. **NTEU's specific position on LMI curriculum tools:** Has the NTEU developed any position specific to data-driven curriculum review tools (vs. its general AI policy)? What conditions would make such a tool acceptable to members?
6. **JSA data licensing for institutions:** Under what terms, cost, and latency does Jobs and Skills Australia make occupation/skills data available to individual universities for curriculum workflows? Any current university agreements?
7. **Accreditation appetite for LMI evidence:** In 2023–25 AACSB CIR or Engineers Australia self-assessments, has quantitative labour market alignment data been used as evidence for Standard 4.1 (or equivalent), and was it accepted by peer review panels?
8. **Faculty attitudes to external skills-gap data:** Among academics presented with commercial LMI data in a program review, what was the predominant response (adoption / reframing / rejection), and what determined it? Does the US "faculty resistance" finding hold in Australia?

## Interview Guide Skeleton

The following questions draw from the open research questions documented in compass-problem-tree.md and the eight priority questions above. Use as a flexible guide — not a script.

**For T&L Directors and curriculum-adjacent roles:**
1. Walk me through what a typical curriculum review process looks like at your institution — who is involved, what triggers it, and what information is consulted?
2. When a faculty member or curriculum team is reviewing a program, do they ever have access to current labour market data — job demand, skills trends, employer signals?
3. If that data exists, where does it come from and how does it reach them?
4. What would it mean for a curriculum team to have real-time intelligence on whether their program is producing graduates with the skills employers are actually hiring for?
5. What would stop that from being useful — trust, format, workflow, culture?
6. What does TEQSA's shift to regulatory oversight in 2026 mean practically for your team?

**For academics who have been through curriculum review:**
1. What triggered the curriculum review you were involved in?
2. What information did you consult — and what did you wish you had?
3. Was there any external signal about graduate employment outcomes in the room?
4. Who had authority to actually change the program, and what did they need to be convinced by?

**For IR/Strategy office contacts:**
1. What labour market data does your institution currently subscribe to?
2. Who uses that data and for what purposes?
3. Has a curriculum team ever come to you for labour market evidence — and if so, what did you give them?
4. What would need to be true for labour market intelligence to be a routine input into curriculum review?

**For vendor contacts:**
1. How does a typical Australian university engagement start — who reaches out, from which department?
2. What are the most common objections you hear from Australian HE buyers?
3. Do you have a product specifically aimed at curriculum design decisions, or is that use case marginal?
