What it measures

A durability score, not another ranking

DFVA — Degree Future-Viability Assessment — scores a program on eleven dimensions (ten core + an Irreplaceability premium), each 0–3, to a /36 total on a four-band scale. It's an independent, evidence-based read that complements accreditation — it does not replace it.

Honest scope

What COMPASS is — and what it isn't

It is
  • An external durability signal for a program
  • A curriculum diagnosis — where to intervene, and the likely effect
  • Evidence-based and comparable across programs
It is not
  • Accreditation, or a substitute for it
  • A ranking or a league table
  • A replacement for academic judgement
Naming the boundary is the point — this earns a place in the conversation, not above it
