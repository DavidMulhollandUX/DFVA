# Evidura — Independence Structure Options

**Prepared for:** Internal discussion — Brad (Director), UoM Commercialisation / Research & Enterprise
**Date:** 2026-06-30
**Status:** Draft for decision
**Context:** DFVA is a curriculum durability assessment methodology developed within Service Experience & Design at UoM. It has been piloted on 41 UoM programs with promising results. The next phase requires charging competitor universities — which demands structural independence from UoM.

---

## The Problem

Evidura's value proposition is *independent, trusted assessment of degree durability.* But it was built inside the University of Melbourne, by a UoM employee, using UoM resources, with UoM programs scored first.

If we ask competitor universities to pay for ratings while this remains true, we will face one of two outcomes:

1. **They refuse to buy** — perceiving the tool as a UoM commercialisation play, not an independent service. The Go8 design partners who participated free will balk at an invoice from their competitor's employee.
2. **They buy, but the product is worthless** — because a rating from a captured assessor carries no credibility. The entire value proposition collapses.

The independence question is not a governance nicety. It is the product.

---

## Three Options

### Option A: Full Spin-Out Now

**What:** Founder resigns from UoM employment. DFVA methodology and brand transferred to a new independent entity (Evidura Pty Ltd). UoM retains no equity, no board seat, no revenue share. Methodology licensed from UoM at arm's length (standard university IP commercialisation terms — royalty or one-time licence fee).

| Factor | Assessment |
|---|---|
| **Trust signal** | Strongest possible. Clean break. No ongoing UoM association. |
| **Time to execute** | 3–6 months (IP transfer, legal entity, licence agreement, employment transition) |
| **Personal risk to founder** | Very high. Full-time employment terminated before revenue exists. |
| **UoM's incentive** | Low. University loses control of IP with no ongoing stake. Commercialisation office may resist. |
| **Funding required** | $300K–$500K seed to cover founder salary + operations through Year 1. |
| **Verdict** | Cleanest trust signal, highest personal risk. Only viable if seed funding is secured first. |

### Option B: Staged Spin-Out (RECOMMENDED)

**What:** Three stages across 12–24 months:

**Stage 1 — Legal Separation (Months 1–6)**
- Evidura Pty Ltd incorporated as a separate legal entity.
- Independent advisory board of 3 seated: one curriculum/L&T expert (non-UoM), one labour-market economist, one governance/ethics specialist. No UoM employees on the board.
- DFVA methodology licensed to Evidura Pty Ltd under a standard UoM IP licence (royalty structure TBD, capped at modest percentage — the licence exists to establish arm's-length transfer, not as a revenue centre).
- Revenue concentration rule codified in the entity's constitution: no single institution >15% of annual revenue.
- Founder remains UoM employee during Stage 1. Evidura operates as a side project with UoM conflict-of-interest disclosure.

**Stage 2 — Operational Separation (Months 6–18)**
- Founder transitions to part-time UoM (0.4–0.6 FTE), part-time Evidura CEO.
- First paying customers secured under the independent entity (not under UoM procurement).
- Methodology governance charter published — detailing scoring protocol, appeals process, revision cycle, and independence safeguards.
- Inter-rater reliability study completed and published (see separate study protocol).
- UoM equity position resolved: recommendation is UoM holds 10–20% equity (standard for university spin-outs) with no board seat and no operational control. Equity exists to acknowledge the incubation investment, not to govern.

**Stage 3 — Full Independence (Months 18–24)**
- Founder resigns from UoM employment. Full-time Evidura CEO.
- UoM's role limited to: equity holder (minority, no board seat), IP licensor (arm's-length royalty), and first customer (paying at standard commercial rates, same as any other Go8 institution).
- Evidura operates from separate premises (not UoM offices).
- All Evidura communications, contracts, and invoicing are from the Pty Ltd — no unimelb.edu.au addresses, no UoM letterhead, no UoM calendar invites.

| Factor | Assessment |
|---|---|
| **Trust signal** | Strong — by Stage 3, structurally identical to Option A. The staged path is visible and verifiable. |
| **Time to execute** | 12–24 months total. The trust signal improves at each stage, not just at the end. |
| **Personal risk to founder** | Manageable. Income preserved through transition. Full risk only at Stage 3, by which point revenue should exist. |
| **UoM's incentive** | Balanced. UoM retains equity upside + IP licence revenue + first-customer status. Commercialisation office views this as a standard spin-out. |
| **Funding required** | $150K–$300K for Stage 1 (legal, incorporation, advisory board). Revenue expected to cover operations by Stage 3. |
| **Verdict** | Recommended. Preserves the trust signal, manages personal risk, gives UoM a stake, and provides a defensible answer to the independence question at every stage. |

### Option C: Stay Internal — Academic Research Only

**What:** DFVA remains a UoM research project. No commercial entity. No competitor university is asked to pay. Methodology published academically. Ratings of UoM programs only. Benchmark dataset built for research purposes.

| Factor | Assessment |
|---|---|
| **Trust signal** | N/A — no commercial claim to defend. |
| **Time to execute** | Immediate. |
| **Personal risk to founder** | Zero. |
| **Revenue** | Zero (grants possible). |
| **Verdict** | Viable if Options A and B are rejected. Not a failure — an academic contribution to the field. But not a venture. |

## The Independence Architecture — Key Design Principles

Whichever option is chosen, these structural elements are non-negotiable for credibility:

1. **No UoM employee rates a competitor's program.** By the time any non-UoM university pays, the founder must not draw a UoM salary.
2. **No UoM board seat.** The independent advisory board (and eventual formal board) must have zero UoM employees.
3. **Published methodology governance charter.** The scoring rubric, revision process, appeals mechanism, and independence safeguards must be public. Transparency is the defense against capture.
4. **Revenue concentration cap.** No single institution >15% of annual revenue. Codified in the entity's constitution.
5. **UoM pays the same rate as any other Go8 university.** No preferential pricing. UoM is a customer, not an owner-operator.
6. **UoM programs are re-rated on the same schedule as competitors.** UoM's first-mover advantage in the benchmark dataset must be addressed — either by re-rating all programs after methodology finalisation, or by transparently disclosing the methodology revision history.

## Risks of the Staged Approach

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Stage 1 trust deficit** — during the 6-month legal-separation stage, the independence question is still live. Design partners may delay commitment. | Frame Stage 1 as "building the independence structure now." No competitor university is asked to pay until the entity exists. Pilot ratings remain free. |
| **UoM Commercialisation delays** — university IP transfer can take 6–12 months. | Start the conversation now. Engage Brad as sponsor. Frame as standard spin-out, not novel arrangement. |
| **Founder burnout** — dual employment for 12–18 months is demanding. | Part-time UoM arrangement must be genuine — not full-time workload on reduced pay. Negotiate workload reduction, not just FTE reduction. |
| **Advisory board recruitment difficulty** — finding 3 credible, willing, independent members. | Start informal approaches now. Target: one from another Go8 (retired DVC-Academic or PVC L&T), one from industry (labour-market economist), one from governance (former TEQSA or AQF panel member). |
| **UoM equity negotiation stalls** — commercialisation office demands >20% or board seat. | Negotiating position: the venture's entire value depends on independence. UoM equity >20% or a board seat destroys the product. UoM's choice is between 10–20% of a viable venture and 100% of an unsellable one. |

## The One-Paragraph Answer for Stakeholders

When asked "what's the relationship between Evidura and the University of Melbourne?" the answer at each stage:

**Stage 1:** *"Evidura is a separate legal entity with an independent advisory board. The methodology was developed at Melbourne and is licensed to Evidura under a standard IP agreement. The founder is managing a disclosed conflict of interest during the transition period. No competitor university has been asked to pay."*

**Stage 2:** *"Evidura operates independently with its own board. Melbourne holds a minority equity position — standard for university spin-outs — with no board seat and no operational control. The founder is transitioning to full-time Evidura CEO. Paying customers are contracted with Evidura Pty Ltd, not the University of Melbourne."*

**Stage 3:** *"Evidura is an independent company. Melbourne is a minority shareholder and a paying customer — same as any other institution we rate. The founder's only employment is Evidura. Our independence is structurally guaranteed."*
