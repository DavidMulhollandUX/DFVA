04 · Synthesis

Where value is created, captured & lost

The MEC questions, answered against the map: where is value created and captured, where is it lost, which areas look promising for a venture — and where are the tensions and untapped potential?

Created

Real value is created upstream, where raw job-market noise is turned into structured, scored intelligence. This is genuine, defensible knowledge work.

Captured

Captured by the LMI vendor at the data & taxonomy layer — high margin, low capital, ~1% churn — and by the CMS owner (TechnologyOne) that holds the curriculum workflow.

Lost

Lost at the break between intelligence and the curriculum decision — and again downstream, borne by graduates in roles that under-use their degree. An unpriced externality.

◆  Promising area

The last mile: translating existing upstream intelligence into a curriculum-ready signal. The data is already produced and paid for; nobody delivers it to the curriculum decision in Australia.

The need is real and the signal already exists — the gap persists only because no player yet translates it into the curriculum decision.

⚖  Tensions to resolve

Demand is latent, not regulated. TEQSA polices assessment integrity, not curriculum-market durability — so adoption needs a DVC champion, not a compliance trigger.

Politics. The NTEU favours staff autonomy — a decision-support tool is compatible; a decision-replacement tool invites disputes.

Gatekeeper. TechnologyOne owns the curriculum workflow with no LMI partner — an unoccupied adjacency, and a make-or-buy question.

◇  Untapped potential

Accreditation pull. AACSB Standard 4.1 (15 AU business schools) and the AMC's 2024 digital-health standard already demand evidence of market-aligned, technology-current curricula.

Council attention. The relevance of the degree and the future of graduate jobs are now live questions at University Council — top-down demand for exactly this evidence.

No incumbent here. Skillabi & Mapademics are US-only; no Australian product exists.
