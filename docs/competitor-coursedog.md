## Executive Summary

Five things COMPASS leadership must know about Coursedog:

**1. Coursedog is a workflow tool, not a viability tool.** It manages the bureaucratic process of curriculum change — approvals, catalog publishing, scheduling. It explicitly cannot assess whether a program should exist, is educationally sound, or is aligned with future labour market demand. This is not a gap they are filling; it is outside their product category entirely.

**2. COMPASS occupies uncontested strategic ground.** No vendor in the curriculum management category — Coursedog, CourseLeaf CIM, Modern Campus Curriculum, or any other — offers curriculum viability scoring, AI displacement risk assessment, or graduate employment alignment in a unified tool. COMPASS has no direct competitor.

**3. Coursedog has zero Australian presence.** Web traffic is 85.82% US. No Australian customers, no AQF/TEQSA positioning, no APAC office, no localisation. The $90M JMI Equity investment was directed at US product development and team buildout, not international expansion. The Australian market is entirely open.

**4. Coursedog is third in its own market despite $112.7M raised.** It holds 14% share in North American curriculum management, behind CourseLeaf CIM (29%) and Modern Campus Curriculum (25%). GetLatka reports $8.5M ARR in 2024. The gap between capital raised and revenue suggests high customer acquisition costs and difficult enterprise sales cycles — not a dominant incumbent.

**5. Coursedog's AI is largely aspirational.** Their December 2024 white paper found only 11% of institutional professionals are actually using AI for academic operations. Coursedog's own AI features are early-stage: scheduling demand forecasting, workflow routing automation, and the April 2026 acquisition of ClassRanked for course evaluation synthesis. There is no AI-driven program viability analysis, no automation exposure scoring, and no labour market intelligence engine. Their AI narrative is a positioning play, not a shipped capability.

## Product

### Core Modules

Coursedog markets itself as an **"Intelligent Academic Operations Platform"** covering six primary product areas:

**1. Curriculum Management**
- No-code drag-and-drop form builder for course and program proposals
- Configurable dynamic approval workflows with email notifications and voting dashboards
- Searchable digital archive of all courses and programs
- Degree Map builder; learning outcomes tracking at each academic level
- Real-time SIS sync (auto-updates catalog, scheduling, and degree audit data)
- Pre-built accreditation reports; automatic version/revision tracking with effective dating
- Syllabus management (centralised creation, institutional branding enforcement)

**2. Academic Course Scheduling**
- Section/class schedule optimizer: automatically tests configurations for course offerings, seat counts, and time blocks against space and demand
- Room and resource optimizer; faculty preference collection and workload balancing
- Course demand projection and capacity optimisation

**3. Online Catalog Management**
- Auto-generates searchable, mobile-accessible catalog from approved curriculum data
- Real-time sync from curriculum proposal workflows to public catalog
- Version control and audit trails

**4. Assessment Management** *(launched January 2024; expanded via ClassRanked acquisition April 2026)*
- Centralised assessment planning and evidence tracking aligned to accreditor requirements
- Post-acquisition: AI-driven course evaluation comment synthesis branded "Assessment Cloud"

**5. Faculty Workload Management** *(launched January 2024)*

**6. Academic Operations Analytics** *(launched January 2024)*
- Enrollment heat maps, instructor workload reports, space utilisation analytics
- Curriculum Analytics product for tracking curriculum data over time

## COMPASS Positioning Against Coursedog

### How to Describe Coursedog to a Prospect

> "Coursedog is an excellent operational platform — it manages the bureaucratic process of curriculum change: approvals, scheduling, catalog publishing. If you want a workflow automation tool, it is a serious option. What it cannot do is tell you whether the curriculum itself is fit for purpose in an AI-economy labour market. Coursedog automates the process of curriculum governance; COMPASS assesses the strategic viability of what you are governing. They answer different questions and they are used by different people in your institution."

### One-Sentence Differentiation

> **"Coursedog automates how curriculum changes get approved; COMPASS scores whether the curriculum itself will survive the next decade of AI-driven labour market disruption."**

### When COMPASS Wins

1. **The prospect is a program director, dean, or QA lead** asking strategic questions — "Is our Master of Science in [X] still viable? Are our graduates employable?" These are not Coursedog's buyers.
2. **The institution is not ready for $100K+ implementation** — COMPASS has no SIS integration requirement and no data migration burden at assessment stage.
3. **The prospect is an Australian or APAC institution** — Coursedog has zero local presence, no AQF/TEQSA positioning, no Australian references. COMPASS has a University of Melbourne prototype.
4. **The prospect needs to justify program investment decisions to a board or funding body** — COMPASS produces a viability score, risk band, and redesign roadmap suited to governance documentation.
5. **The prospect is concerned about AI disruption to their programs** — Coursedog has nothing to say here. COMPASS's automation exposure dimension is the only product in the category that addresses it.
6. **Speed matters** — COMPASS produces an assessment from a handbook URL in minutes vs. Coursedog's 10-month procurement cycle.

### When COMPASS Faces Harder Selling

1. **The prospect already runs Coursedog successfully** — position as complementary, not competitive; different buyers, different questions.
2. **The primary pain is workflow inefficiency** — if the need is "our approval process is broken" or "eliminate PDF catalogs," COMPASS does not address that.
3. **Deep SIS integration is required** — COMPASS's current URL-based model does not replace Coursedog's data infrastructure role.
4. **The prospect is a large US institution in the Ellucian ecosystem** — Coursedog's 140+ Banner connections are a real switching cost barrier in operational tools.
5. **Procurement requires an established vendor track record** — Coursedog has 300+ customers and $112.7M raised; COMPASS is a prototype.
