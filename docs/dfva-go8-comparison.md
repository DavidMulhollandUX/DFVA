# Go8 Comparative Analysis — DFVA Benchmarking

**Date:** 2026-06-10 | **Methodology:** Same DFVA rubric applied to handbook/course page content via Hermes LLM

---

## Methodology

Equivalent programs at USyd, UNSW, and ANU were scored using the same 11-dimension DFVA rubric. Content was extracted from university course pages via web_extract. Note: Go8 universities vary significantly in the depth of publicly available curriculum detail. Scores are indicative — UniMelb's handbook provides richer detail than most peers, which may inflate relative scores.

## Analysis

### Where UniMelb Leads

| Dimension | UniMelb Advantage | Evidence |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| Outcome Evidence | +1-2 pts vs peers | QILT integration gives UniMelb programs evidence-backed D10 scores. Most Go8 peers publish no graduate employment data on course pages. |
| Curriculum Currency (CS) | +1 vs USyd | UniMelb's CS program scored higher on currency than USyd's lighter course page |
| Systems Thinking (CS) | +1 vs peers | UniMelb CS's integrated systems approach scored higher than USyd/UNSW's modular structures |

### Where Peers Lead

| University | Advantage | Evidence |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| UNSW (IT) | AI Literacy +3 | UNSW's MIT has an AI specialisation AND explicit industry AI partnerships. UniMelb CS has no dedicated AI core. |
| UNSW (Clin Psych) | Research infrastructure | PhD/Masters combined pathway, cutting-edge labs, forensic psychology workshops |
| UNSW (IT) | Career data | Published Seek projections (Cyber Analyst 38.9% growth) directly on course page |

### Key Finding: Go8 CS/IT programs vary dramatically

The 10-point gap between UNSW MIT (27) and USyd MCompSci (19) reflects fundamentally different levels of public curriculum detail, not necessarily program quality. USyd's course page is marketing-focused with minimal curriculum structure visible. UNSW's is comprehensive with specialisation details, industry partnerships, and outcome data.

**This is itself a finding:** public curriculum transparency correlates with higher DFVA scores because the rubric rewards *evidence.* Programs that publish detailed curriculum, career outcomes, and industry engagement data score higher — even if the underlying program quality is similar.

---

## Recommendations

1. **UniMelb should adopt UNSW's AI specialisation model** for CS/IT programs. A dedicated AI specialisation would close UniMelb's AI Literacy gap (currently 1 vs UNSW's 3).

2. **Publish career outcome data on course pages.** UNSW's inclusion of Seek growth projections is a model. UniMelb already has the QILT data — it just needs to be surfaced publicly.

3. **Note the transparency effect.** UniMelb's handbook provides richer public detail than most Go8 peers. This inflates UniMelb's relative scores. A fair comparison would require access to internal curriculum documents for all universities.

4. **Expand benchmarking to 10 programs.** This pilot covered 2 fields. A full Go8 comparison across Engineering, Law, Business, and Education would provide more robust insights.

---

**Limitations:** Content extracted from public course pages only — not full handbooks. USyd and UQ course pages returned minimal content. ANU returned 404. Scores should be treated as indicative. Methodological note: UniMelb's handbook provides significantly richer public detail than most Go8 peers, which may inflate its relative DFVA position.
