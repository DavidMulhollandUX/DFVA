# COMPASS — Faculty of Science Meeting: Question Guide

**Purpose:** this is a *discovery* meeting, not a sales pitch. You have scored the Faculty of Science's 11 programs; the deliverables (one-pager, dashboard, rollup, deck) are **props to provoke a reaction**, not slides to lecture from. The goal is to learn how curriculum decisions actually get made here and to find the strongest reasons COMPASS *shouldn't* exist — before you spend years finding out the hard way.

Built from the Validate pack: hypotheses **H1–H4** ([compass-validate.md](compass-validate.md)), falsifiable questions **FQ1–8** ([research-plan.md](research-plan.md)), interview guides A–E, the stakeholder profiles ([compass-stakeholder-analysis.md](compass-stakeholder-analysis.md)), and the Mom-Test discipline ([agent-harness.md](agent-harness.md) §17.3).

## 0. The load-bearing bets you are here to test

| # | Bet | You are *wrong* if… | Who tests it best |
|---|-----|---------------------|-------------------|
| **H1 — Demand** | Decision-makers genuinely *want* an external durability signal they can be held to | "We'd never let an outside number grade our degrees" | Dean / Associate Dean, Program Director |
| **H2 — Standing** | An *independent* signal can earn enough authority to become *expected* | "Only TEQSA / a peak body could confer that — a startup score would be ignored" | Associate Dean, any accreditation-facing academic |
| **H3 — Buyer/budget** | A recognised budget line exists for curriculum intelligence | "There's no budget line; it comes out of someone's hide" | Associate Dean (Education), faculty GM |
| **H4 — Unoccupied gap** | The labour-market-intelligence → curriculum gap is genuinely unoccupied | "Our LMS/careers/IR already does this" | Careers, IR, T&L |

**Decision rule (after ~4–5 conversations):** COMPASS survives if **H1 *and* H2 hold** under pressure *and* at least one of **H3/H4** holds. If H1 or H2 fails → loop back (this is a *good* outcome, it saves years). If H3 fails but H1/H2 hold → reshape go-to-market. If H4 fails → pivot to partner-with posture.

## 1. Mom-Test discipline — read this before you walk in

The enemy is politeness. Academics will say supportive things; those sound like validation and are worthless as evidence. Five rules:

1. **Talk about their world, not your idea.** Frame it as "I'm trying to understand how program review actually works here," not "I built a tool that scores programs." The moment you pitch, the data is contaminated.
2. **Ask about the past, not the future.** Not "would you use a durability score?" (invites a free yes) but "walk me through the last program review you sat in — what evidence was actually on the table?" (yields fact).
3. **Anchor on commitments already made — money, time, reputation.** "Have you ever fought for budget / spent your own time getting labour-market evidence into a review? What happened?"
4. **Treat compliments and hypotheticals as noise.** "That's really useful" is a *red flag, not a milestone*. Steer back: "when did you last hit that problem?"
5. **Go in to be falsified.** If everyone "validates" it, you asked leading questions.

**Open with this (verbatim-ish):** *"I'm not here to sell anything — I'm trying to find the reasons this shouldn't exist. I've run our assessment over the faculty's eleven programs and I'd value you trying to talk me out of it."*

**Always close with:** (a) *"What didn't I ask that I should have?"* (b) *"Who else should I be speaking to?"* (referrals make outreach self-driving) (c) *"If you were betting, would you bet against us? Why?"*

## 2. Ask-the-room — role-tuned question sets

Each block is **Learn → Challenge → Probe** and tagged with the bet/FQ it tests. Pull the *cares/frustrations* line first — it tells you what will land.

### 2A. Dean / Associate Dean (Education / Teaching & Learning) — tests **H1, H2, H3**
*Cares most about: program quality + demonstrating labour-market alignment (Accord); frustrated by AI moving faster than policy, and curriculum revision eating ~200 hrs of senior academic time. Power: high — holds the education budget line.*

**Learn**
- Walk me through how a program review starts in the faculty — what triggers it, who's in the room, what evidence gets consulted?
- What's actually changing in how you think about program relevance right now (Accord, TEQSA, AI)?

**Challenge** *(H1)*
- When a Science program is reviewed, does *current labour-market data* ever reach the table — and if not, why not?
- The mental model that "accredited / strong discipline = future-proof" — is that fair, or would a hard external durability score be unwelcome?
- Would you let an *independent* number grade your degrees' AI durability? What would make that intolerable? *(H2)*

**Probe** *(H3)*
- Is there a *budget line* for curriculum intelligence, distinct from what careers/IR already spend? Who signs, and up to what amount?
- What evidence would convince you this is worth solving — and what would convince you it isn't?

### 2B. Program Director / course coordinator (e.g. an MSc stream lead) — tests **H1, FQ8**
*Cares most about: learning outcomes, AQF/TEQSA compliance, student experience. Frustrated by: admin overload on top of teaching; little structural power; curriculum work deprioritised vs research. They are the USER, rarely the BUYER.*

**Learn**
- What triggered the last review/refresh of your program, and what did the process feel like day-to-day?
- What information did you consult — and what did you *wish* you'd had?

**Challenge** *(FQ8)*
- Was there *any* external signal about graduate outcomes in the room, or was it internal (peer benchmarking, accreditation)?
- If someone handed you commercial skills-gap data mid-review, would you have trusted it, reframed it, or rejected it — and what decides that?

**Probe**
- Who actually had authority to change the program, and what did they need to be convinced by?
- Where was the friction a tool could never fix?

### 2C. T&L centre / academic developer / learning designer — tests **FQ8, burden**
*Cares most about: supporting redesign day-to-day, what faculty actually ask for. Frustrated by: being positioned as policy-implementers, not decision-shapers.*

**Learn**
- When a Science program team comes to you for a redesign, what do they bring, and what do they ask for first?
- What does a "good" curriculum-evidence pack look like to you?

**Challenge**
- Have they *ever* arrived with labour-market evidence? What happened to it?
- Where does most redesign time actually go — and would an external diagnosis save or add work?

**Probe**
- What would make an external durability read genuinely useful at the point you're brought in — and what would make it just noise?

### 2D. Careers / Institutional Research (if present) — tests **H4, FQ3, FQ6**
*Cares most about: data that executives act on; moving from reporting to advisory. Frustrated by: data silos; their analysis not being acted on.*

**Learn**
- What labour-market / outcomes data does the faculty or university subscribe to, and who uses it for what?
- Has a curriculum team ever come to you for labour-market evidence? Walk me through what happened.

**Challenge** *(H4)*
- Why doesn't that data cross into curriculum decisions today — organisational, technical, cultural, or just nobody's job?
- If you handed a Science program team a skills-gap report tomorrow, what would actually happen to it?

**Probe**
- What would have to be true for labour-market intelligence to become a *routine* input to curriculum review?

## 3. Artifact-reaction prompts — show it, then watch what they *do*

The reaction is the data. For each prop: show → ask → watch-for → kill-signal. (Don't explain the methodology unless asked — watch the unprompted move.)

| Prop (WS-B deliverable) | Show & ask | Watch for (validates) | Kill-signal (refutes) | Tests |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Science one-pager** (portfolio read) | "Here's the faculty's eleven programs on one page. What's your first reaction?" | They point to a specific program, ask "how was X scored?", or say "I'd take this to [committee/colleague]" | Polite nod; "interesting"; no reach for a decision; "the discipline protects us" | H1, EXP-01/07 |
| **Portfolio dashboard** (per-program radar) | "This is the dimension fingerprint for [their program]. Does it match how you'd describe it?" | They dispute or confirm a specific dimension — engagement with the *content* | They dispute the *right to score* it at all | H1, H2 |
| **Improvement-priorities rollup** | "If the faculty added AI literacy across these, this is the collective shift. Would this have helped your last review?" | "Yes — and I'd have wanted it *here*…"; asks who'd own it | "We already do this"; "wouldn't change anything" | H1, EXP-05 |
| **Accreditation framing** (in the deck/one-pager footnote) | "Could a panel treat this as evidence under TEQSA / your accreditation?" | "Maybe, if it came with X / from Y" — a path to standing | "Only the regulator's word counts" | H2, EXP-03 |

## 4. Self-prep — sharpen your own understanding (answer these cold before the meeting)

These are the questions a sharp Dean will put to *you*. If you can't answer crisply, that's the work.

- **The honest boundary statement.** In one sentence: what COMPASS *is* (an external durability signal + curriculum diagnosis) and what it is *not* (not accreditation, not a ranking, not a replacement for academic judgement). Say this early — it earns the room.
- **What would falsify H1?** ("If the Dean says curriculum review never consults external LMI and never would, demand is rhetorical.") Be able to name it.
- **What would falsify H2?** ("If every path to standing runs only through TEQSA/peak bodies, an independent signal can't become expected.")
- **The amber cells** (from the market-signals scorecard): Is the AU serviceable market deep enough, or only viable globally? Is the moat the *data* (which you don't own) or the *assessment layer*? Who actually signs, and is there a budget line distinct from existing LMI spend? Know your honest answer to each.
- **Why now?** "Haven't we heard 'align to industry' for 20 years?" — your answer: AI is restructuring job *families* on 12–18-month cycles (post-2023); TEQSA named AI 2024–25; the Accord set a workforce-alignment mandate. The *need* is freshly acute even if the pitch sounds old.
- **The Science-specific truth you must not oversell.** All 11 programs are MODERATE, Domain Depth is a perfect 3.0 — these are *strong* programs. The gap is narrow and specific (AI Literacy 1.1; Outcome Evidence 1.1, the latter mostly a data-folding gap). Don't frame it as "your degrees are failing" — frame it as "two fixable dimensions stand between strong and resilient."
