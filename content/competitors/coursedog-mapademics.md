## DFVA's Differentiators

### 1. Methodology Depth vs. Data Overlay

Coursedog/Mapademics overlays LMI data onto existing curriculum metadata. DFVA scores curriculum *design* against 10 dimensions including AI Literacy, Interdisciplinary Integration, Decision Ownership, and Future-Readiness — dimensions LMI data alone cannot assess.

### 2. Independent Standard

Coursedog is an SIS-integrated vendor. Its LMI is a feature of a larger platform sale. DFVA is methodology-first — the scoring rubric is published, citable, and independent of any SIS vendor. This matters for universities that want vendor-neutral quality assurance.

### 3. AI-Durability vs. Job-Market Snapshot

Mapademics' LMI shows *current* job market alignment. DFVA's rubric includes forward-looking dimensions: automation exposure trajectory, AI literacy depth, and systems thinking — capabilities that indicate durability through market shifts, not just alignment with today's jobs.

### 4. Accreditation-Ready Outputs

DFVA produces a 2027 Graduate Profile, prioritised recommendations table with effort ratings, and threshold questions suitable for curriculum committee review and accreditation evidence. Coursedog/Mapademics produces career alignment reports — useful but not structured for academic governance.

## Response Strategy

1. **Don't react.** DFVA's methodology is deeper. Lead with it.
2. **Name the difference.** "AI-durability scoring" vs "labour market data overlay" — make the distinction explicit.
3. **Accelerate H2.** The independent-standard narrative is DFVA's moat. Method page, citations, published rubric.
4. **Monitor.** If Coursedog adds rubric-based assessment (unlikely — they're a workflow company), reassess.
